## 1. Best Practices Applied

This ICP follows frameworks from Lincoln Murphy, Lenny Rachitsky, and others (see `icp-research-notes.md`):

- **3 defining attributes** per segment (Lenny's rule: every successful B2B startup lands on ≥3)
- **Situational framing** (Lincoln Murphy): this ICP is for the next 6 months of GTM, not forever
- **Company-level first, personas second** — ICP defines the org, buyer persona is separate
- **Anti-personas defined** — equally important to know who to reject
- **Scoring dimensions** for qualification — not binary, but graduated fit
- **Built-in validation plan** — this is a hypothesis until proven with data

## 2. Primary ICP: Small Boutique Consultancies (5-50 people)

### Three Defining Attributes
1. **Small consultancy firm** (5-50 people) doing strategy, operations, management, or advisory work
2. **Team creates client-facing slide decks weekly** as a core deliverable across multiple engagements
3. **Uses PowerPoint or Google Slides today** with no internal design team

### Firmographics
| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **Company size** | 5-50 people (typically 5-30 consultants + support staff) |
| **Revenue** | $1M-$20M/year |
| **Industry** | Management consulting, strategy advisory, business operations, financial advisory |
| **Geography** | English-speaking markets (US, UK, Canada, Australia) — start here |
| **Business model** | Project-based / retainer client work |
| **Stage** | Established (3+ years), growing client base |

### Technographics
- Primary tools: PowerPoint, Google Slides
- Uses cloud productivity (Google Workspace or Microsoft 365)
- Comfortable with SaaS (uses project management, CRM, collaboration tools)
- No internal design team or dedicated slide production resources
- NOT using Beautiful.ai, Gamma, or Tome yet (or tried and found them too consumer/generic)

### Pain Points
- **Inconsistent slide quality across the team** — each consultant builds differently
- **No brand consistency** — decks look different depending on who made them
- **No design team** — can't justify a full-time designer, but need polished output
- **Multiple consultants spending 5-15 hours/week each on slides** — compound time waste
- Needs "consulting-grade" output (structured, data-driven, McKinsey/BCG aesthetic) — consumer AI tools produce pretty but unstructured slides
- Senior partners waste time reviewing/fixing junior consultants' formatting instead of content
- Onboarding new consultants to firm's slide standards is painful

### Psychographics & Buying Signals
- Values professional, consistent brand appearance across all client deliverables
- Team economics: $30-50/seat/mo easily justified when each consultant saves 5+ hours/week
- Managing Partner actively looking for tools to improve team productivity and output quality
- Firm is growing — adding consultants means the slide quality problem gets worse, not better
- Posts on LinkedIn about consulting operations, team building, delivery excellence

### Buyer Persona: "Managing Partner Marcus"
- **Title:** Managing Partner / Founding Partner / Head of Delivery
- **Age:** 35-55
- **Background:** Ex-Big 4 or ex-MBB who started their own firm
- **Day in the life:** Client relationships → team oversight → quality review → business development
- **Success metric:** Team utilization, client satisfaction, firm profitability, brand reputation
- **Budget authority:** Full or shared with 1-2 co-founders
- **Decision process:** Evaluates tool personally, trials with 2-3 team members, rolls out if it works
- **Key objection:** "Will my team actually adopt it?" — needs to be easier than current workflow

### Why Primary (Aligned with ICP Scoring Model)
Per the three-circle scoring framework (Feb 10):
- **Firmographic fit:** Consulting industry (+30), 5-50 employees (sweet spot), English-speaking market
- **Higher LTV:** Multi-seat deals vs single-user; natural expansion as firm grows
- **Stronger retention:** Team adoption creates switching costs; embedded in firm workflow
- **Word-of-mouth:** Consultants move between firms → carry tool recommendations

## 3. Secondary ICP Segments

### 3A: Mid-Size Consultancies (50-200 people) — Rank #2

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **Size** | 50-200 employees |
| **Revenue** | $10M-$100M/year |
| **Pain** | Same team consistency problems as primary, amplified at scale; brand enforcement across offices/teams |
| **Buyer** | COO, Head of Operations, or Managing Partner |
| **Decision** | Longer — may involve brief procurement review, needs champion internally |
| **Value prop** | Highest LTV (20-50+ seats); team-wide consistency; natural expansion from primary segment |
| **Why secondary:** | Likely needs features we don't have yet (SSO, admin controls, usage analytics, team management). Higher support/onboarding expectations. Volume discount negotiations. |
| **When to pursue:** | After product-market fit with primary; when team features are built |

### 3B: Corporate Strategy / Internal Consulting Teams (3-15 people) — Rank #3

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **Size** | 3-15 person internal strategy team within larger company |
| **Revenue** | N/A (budget within larger org) |
| **Pain** | Same slide-building burden but inside corporate; constrained by brand guidelines |
| **Buyer** | VP Strategy or Chief of Staff |
| **Value prop** | Multi-seat deal, embedded in team workflow |
| **Why secondary:** | Procurement complexity, IT security reviews, longer cycle — pursue after product-market fit |

### 3C: Solo / Micro-Consultancies (1-5 people) — Rank #4

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **Size** | 1-5 people (solo consultant or micro-firm) |
| **Revenue** | $100K-$500K/year |
| **Pain** | Spends 5-15 hours/week building slides; starts from blank; can't afford a designer |
| **Buyer** | The consultant themselves — full budget authority |
| **Decision** | Fast — tries free trial, converts if it saves time in first session |
| **Value prop** | Time savings, consulting-grade quality without design skills |
| **Why secondary:** | Lower LTV (single seat), higher churn risk, price-sensitive. But fast to acquire and great for early product feedback, viral loop, and word-of-mouth |
| **Strategic role:** | Awareness/growth channel rather than core revenue segment |

### 3D: Freelance Business Analysts / Startup Advisors — Rank #5

| Dimension | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **Size** | Solo |
| **Pain** | Needs investor decks, board decks, strategy presentations — but less frequently |
| **Why secondary:** | Lowest frequency of use = lowest willingness to pay recurring. Nice-to-have awareness channel |

## 4. Anti-Personas (Who is NOT Our Customer)

### ❌ Do NOT pursue:

| Anti-Persona | Why Not |
|-------------|---------|
| **Students / academics** | No budget, low-stakes presentations, won't pay |
| **Enterprise sales teams** | Need CRM-integrated pitch decks, not strategy slides; different product |
| **Marketing/creative teams** | Want design-first tools (Canva, Figma); aesthetics > structure |
| **Keynote speakers / trainers** | Need speaker-support slides (big text, images), not data-dense consulting decks |
| **Large MBB / Big 4 firms** | Have internal design teams, proprietary tools, and would never use external SaaS for client work |
| **Non-English markets (for now)** | Template/AI quality depends on English; expand later |
| **Price-sensitive hobbyists** | Looking for free tools; will churn at any price point |
| **One-time deck builders** | Need a single pitch deck ever; won't subscribe monthly |

### Red Flags in Conversations
- "I only make 1-2 presentations per year" → too infrequent
- "We have a design team for that" → don't need us
- "Can I just pay once?" → wrong business model fit (ironic given course pivot — but SaaS needs recurring value)
- "I need it to look exactly like [specific brand template]" → custom design work, not our product

## 5. Qualifying Criteria / ICP Fit Score

Score prospects 0-5 on each dimension. **Target: ≥ 15/25 for primary ICP.**

| Dimension | 5 (Perfect fit) | 3 (Okay fit) | 1 (Poor fit) |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Deck frequency** | Team creates 5+ decks/week | 2-4 decks/week | < 1 deck/month |
| **Deck type** | Strategy/consulting (data-heavy, structured) | Business/internal | Creative/marketing |
| **Current tool** | PowerPoint/GSlides (no design team) | Already uses AI tool (switchable) | Design tool (Figma, Canva) |
| **Team size** | 5-50 (consultancy) | Solo or 2-4 | 50+ (enterprise) |
| **Budget authority** | Partner/founder (full or 1 co-sign) | Needs committee approval | Procurement required |

### Quick Qualification Questions (for sales/discovery calls)
1. "How many presentations do you create in a typical month?"
2. "What tool do you use today? What frustrates you about it?"
3. "How much time do you spend on formatting vs. content?"
4. "Who decides on new tools — you, or someone else?"
5. "What does a 'great' presentation look like for your clients?"

## 6. Validation Plan

This ICP is a **hypothesis**. Here's how to validate it with real data:

### Immediate (Weeks 1-4)
- [ ] **Outbound test:** Send 2 versions of cold outreach on LinkedIn — one targeting solo consultants, one targeting boutique firms. Measure response rates.
- [ ] **Landing page A/B:** Create separate landing pages for "solo consultant" vs "consultancy team" positioning. Drive traffic via targeted LinkedIn ads. Measure signup conversion.
- [ ] **Community listening:** Monitor r/consulting, consulting Twitter, and LinkedIn for pain-point language around slide creation. Document exact phrases for copy.
- [ ] **Founder conversations:** Mike conducts 10-15 discovery calls with ex-colleagues and network contacts who fit primary ICP. Use Mom Test methodology.

### Short-term (Months 1-3)
- [ ] **Analyze course buyers in Attio:** Profile existing $149 course customers — do they match this ICP? Which segments had highest completion/satisfaction?
- [ ] **PostHog behavioral data:** When product launches, track which user segments show highest activation (first deck created in < 30 min)
- [ ] **Stripe cohort analysis:** Compare retention/upgrade rates across ICP segments
- [ ] **Win/loss tracking:** For every signup and churn, tag ICP fit score and reason

### Ongoing (Quarterly)
- [ ] **Revisit ICP every quarter** — refine based on actual customer data
- [ ] **Track ICP-fit vs. metrics:** Correlate fit score with LTV, NPS, activation rate
- [ ] **Anti-persona validation:** Confirm that rejected segments indeed show lower retention

### Key Metrics to Watch
| Metric | ICP-fit benchmark (target) |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Trial → Paid conversion | > 15% for ICP-fit users |
| Time to first deck | < 30 min for ICP-fit users |
| Monthly active days | > 8 days/month |
| 90-day retention | > 60% |
| NPS | > 40 |

## Summary

**SlideHeroes' ideal customer is a small boutique consultancy (5-50 people) where multiple consultants create client-facing slide decks weekly using PowerPoint/Google Slides, struggle with inconsistent quality across the team, and lack a design team to enforce brand standards.**

They are Ready (pain compounds with every new hire), Willing (managing partner actively looking to improve team output), and Able (budget authority, SaaS-comfortable, multi-seat deal economics work).

Solo consultants are a strong secondary segment — fast to acquire, great for product feedback — but the primary GTM focus is small consultancies where team adoption drives higher LTV and retention.

This is where we start. We refine from here.
