# Council Debate: Is Santa Claus Real?

## Consensus
Santa Claus is real as a **cultural and psychological phenomenon** — not as a literal physical being, but as a powerful social construct that shapes behavior, meaning, and generosity.

## Key Arguments
- **Literally:** No single immortal man at North Pole delivering billions of gifts
- **Culturally:** Exists as shared narrative transcending geography and generations
- **Psychologically:** Children experience Santa as real through belief; produces tangible effects
- **Philosophically:** Real like money, nations, justice — not natural objects, but social constructs with measurable effects

## Synthesis
Santa exists in the same category as hope, love, and justice — ideas that shape how people move through the world. The story persists because it serves something deeper: the need for magic, mystery, and a figure who gives without expectation of return.
