# Partnerships + affiliates outreach strategy (draft)

Goal: drive qualified top-of-funnel and credibility for SlideHeroes via partners who already reach (a) founders/operators, (b) product/marketing teams, (c) consultants, (d) students & early-career professionals who need presentation skills.

This doc is intended to be a practical playbook: partner types → offer → outreach → tracking → targets.

## 1) Partner programs (what we’re actually offering)

### A. Affiliate program (simple, scalable)
**Offer:** revenue share for paid subscriptions / course purchases.
- Commission: start at **20–30%** of first payment (or **15–20% recurring for 6–12 months**). Keep it simple initially: one plan.
- Cookie window: **30–60 days**.
- Payout: monthly, minimum threshold (e.g., $50).
- Assets: 3–5 landing pages, email swipe copy, short demo video, logos.
- Rules: no bidding on brand keywords; no misleading claims.

**Tracking:** use a dedicated affiliate platform (Rewardful, FirstPromoter, Tolt, PartnerStack-lite) or a Stripe-first solution if applicable.

### B. Content swaps / lesson swaps (credibility + list growth)
**Offer:** co-created content where both parties promote.
- Example: “How to pitch your idea clearly” guest lesson inside partner’s course, and partner provides a guest lesson for SlideHeroes.
- Distribution: newsletter swap + community post + webinar.

**Primary KPI:** email captures + demo signups + assisted conversions.

### C. Tool partnerships (integration + marketplace)
**Offer:** joint landing page + integration value prop.
- Examples: Notion, Airtable, Miro, Figma, Google Slides, PowerPoint add-ins, Loom.
- For presentation-specific tools (Beautiful.ai etc.), treat carefully (they may be competitors) — frame as complementary (training + content quality vs. tooling).

### D. Embedded training / B2B enablement bundles
**Offer:** partner sells SlideHeroes as an add-on for their customers.
- Bundle with incubators/accelerators, co-working spaces, entrepreneurship programs, consulting groups.
- Pricing: per-seat or cohort-based.

## 2) Who to target (partner categories + examples)

### 1) Complementary course providers
**Why:** same learner profile; high trust.
- Entrepreneurship, product marketing, sales, analytics storytelling, consulting.
- Examples (illustrative): indie course creators, cohort-based programs, bootcamps.

**Partner pitch:** “Your students need to communicate their work; we provide the presentation layer.”

### 2) Communities & orgs
**Why:** concentrated audiences; recurring cohorts.
- AIESEC, student entrepreneurship clubs, accelerators, founder communities, PM communities.

**Partner pitch:** “We’ll run a workshop + give your members a perk; you promote it.”

### 3) Agencies / freelancers (consultants who present for clients)
**Why:** they influence spend; they need repeatable training.
- Pitch deck agencies, fractional CMOs, RevOps consultants.

**Partner pitch:** “Add SlideHeroes to your client toolkit; earn referral revenue.”

### 4) Tools & platforms
**Why:** marketplace visibility and credibility.
- Potentially: Beautiful.ai, SlideGenius, pitch deck design services, etc.

**Partner pitch:** “We increase successful usage of your tool by improving story/clarity.”

## 3) The partner value proposition (what’s in it for them)

### Core benefits
- Higher conversion / outcomes for their audience (presentations are universal bottleneck)
- New value add without building a new product
- Revenue share
- Co-marketing exposure

### Concrete deliverables we can offer
- 60-minute live workshop (monthly)
- A reusable “Presentation Clarity Checklist” PDF co-branded
- A “before/after” teardown video
- Discount code for their audience
- Affiliate link + dashboard

## 4) Outreach process (practical workflow)

### Step 0 — shortlist and scoring
Create a spreadsheet with:
- Partner type, audience overlap, distribution size, decision-maker, contact channel, prior relationship
- **Score 1–5** for: overlap, reach, ease, strategic value

Start with 20 targets.

### Step 1 — 3 outreach templates

**Template A: creator/course owner (content swap)**
Subject: Guest lesson for your students: “{topic}”

Hi {Name} — I love your work on {specific}. We’re building SlideHeroes to help {audience} turn messy thinking into clear, persuasive presentations.

Could we do a simple content swap?
- I’ll deliver a guest lesson/workshop for your students: {specific promise}
- You share {asset/webinar/newsletter} with my audience

If it’s interesting, I can send a 1-page outline + 2 sample teardown clips.

— {Sender}

**Template B: community/accelerator (workshop + perk)**
Subject: Free workshop for {Community}: “{title}”

Hi {Name} — quick idea for {Community}. I can run a practical workshop on {title} (60 min) + give members a {discount/perk}.

We handle the content + registration page; you just promote it.

Want me to propose 2 dates?

**Template C: consultant/agency (affiliate + bundle)**
Subject: Add a training layer to your client deliverables

Hi {Name} — your clients likely struggle with {exec storytelling / pitch clarity}. We’ve got a training + tooling workflow for presentation clarity.

If you refer clients, we can:
- provide a bundle price for your engagements
- share revenue via an affiliate link

Open to a quick 15-min chat?

### Step 2 — call script (15 minutes)
- 2 minutes: their audience + problems
- 3 minutes: what SlideHeroes does + outcomes
- 5 minutes: partnership model options (swap, affiliate, workshop)
- 3 minutes: next step (pilot)
- 2 minutes: confirm tracking + success metric

### Step 3 — pilot
Run a **single pilot** per partner:
- one webinar OR one newsletter promotion OR one in-course lesson
- measure signups → activation → paid

### Step 4 — scale
If pilot works, convert to:
- ongoing affiliate, and/or
- quarterly co-marketing, and/or
- annual bundled deal.

## 5) Metrics + decision rules
Track per partner:
- Clicks, leads, activation rate, paid conversion, CAC (if any spend), LTV estimate
- Qualitative: audience fit, inbound quality, support burden

Decision rule:
- Keep if ≥ X paid conversions in 30 days OR clear evidence of high-quality leads.
