## Philosophy

Three principles govern our outbound:

1. **Signal-based selling** (Brendan Short): "Timing is 10x more important than copy. Maybe 100x." Only reach out when you have a specific reason, today.

2. **Permission-based selling** (Anton Maker): Never pitch without permission. Ask consent at every step. If they say no, stop.

3. **Show Me You Know Me** (Sam McKenna): Every touchpoint must prove you did your homework. Be in the 8% who personalize effectively.

**The question every outreach must answer:**
> "What is the specific reason I'm reaching out to this person, today?"

**Timeline expectation:** 300 days minimum commitment before expecting compound results (Anton Maker). This is a long game.

## Phase 1: LinkedIn Outbound (Current)

LinkedIn is our primary outbound channel. Cold email is Phase 2 — only after LinkedIn is running and we have domain reputation.

**Why LinkedIn first:**
- Mike's ex-Mastercard credibility is a trust asset on LinkedIn
- Consultants live on LinkedIn — it's where they buy
- No domain reputation or email infrastructure needed
- C-suite posts get 4X engagement vs company pages (McKenna)
- You don't need a massive following — Alex Boyd generated $5.4M with ~12K followers

### 1. Profile Optimization

Before any outreach, Mike's LinkedIn profile must be optimized as a landing page, not a résumé.

**Checklist** (Anton Maker):
- [ ] **Headline:** Specific who + outcome + proof — NOT job title
  - ❌ "CEO at SlideHeroes"
  - ✅ "Helping consultancies win more pitches with AI-powered presentations | Ex-Mastercard"
- [ ] **Banner:** Positioning + value prop + social proof
- [ ] **About section:** Open with the prospect's problem, not your journey
- [ ] **Experience:** Show results and numbers, not responsibilities
- [ ] **Featured section:** Case studies and customer results, not motivational content
- [ ] **Recent posts:** 8-9 on core expertise, 1-2 adjacent/personal

### 2. Content Strategy

**"Engagement and leads are two different games. Most founders play the wrong one."** — Anton Maker

Don't chase likes. Create content that attracts people with the exact problem you solve.

#### Content Mix — 70-20-10 Rule (Anton Maker)

| Category | % | Purpose |
|----------|---|---------|
| **Problem Awareness** | 70% | Make prospects realize the problem exists or is worse than they think |
| **Solution Education** | 20% | Show different approaches without pitching your product |
| **Product/Service** | 10% | Case studies and specific results |

#### 4-Pillar Content System (Anton Maker)

| Pillar | Weight | What It Is | SlideHeroes Example |
|--------|--------|------------|---------------------|
| **Tactical** | 40% | How-tos, actionable tips | "3 slides every consulting proposal needs" |
| **Transformational** | 20% | Case studies, before/after | "How [firm] cut proposal time from 2 weeks to 2 days" |
| **Insightful** | 20% | Hot takes, contrarian opinions | "Why your pitch deck is losing you deals (and it's not the content)" |
| **Personal** | 20% | Stories, behind-the-scenes | "What I learned about presentations in 20 years at Mastercard" |

**Implementation:** Define 3 topics per pillar = 12 content ideas ready. Batch weekly.

#### 5 Post Formulas That Book Meetings (Anton Maker)

1. **The Transformation Post** — Metric before → after + counterintuitive approach
2. **The Expensive Mistake Post** — What we tried → Why it failed → What actually worked
3. **The Diagnosis Post** — "If [situation], you don't have [symptom], you have [root cause]"
4. **The Hidden Cost Post** — Quantify a cost they're not tracking + your solution's impact
5. **The A vs B Post** — Common approach vs your approach with results comparison

#### Content Principles

- **Curiosity gap** (Alex Boyd): Leave the "how" out of case studies to drive DMs. "Waft the food, don't spear them."
- **Quality over frequency**: 1 great post/week beats 5 mediocre ones
- **Personal profile > company page**: People buy from people, not brands
- **Hyper-specific** (Anton Maker): Generic content attracts generic audiences. Only people with exact problems should relate to your posts.

#### Cadence

- **Minimum:** 2 posts/week
- **Target:** 3-4 posts/week
- **Engagement time:** Spend more time commenting on target prospects' posts than creating your own (Alex Boyd)

### 3. Signal Taxonomy — What Triggers Outreach

**Rule:** Never reach out without a signal. No signal = no outreach.

#### Tier 1 — Trigger-Based Signals (Anton Maker)

These are people who've already shown interest in YOU. Highest conversion.

| Signal | Priority | Expected Response Rate | Action |
|--------|----------|----------------------|--------|
| Content shares | Highest | 50-60% | DM with value-add |
| Post comments | High | 45-55% | DM acknowledging their point |
| Profile views | Medium | 30-40% | Connection request + note |
| Post likes | Lower | 30-35% | Connection request |
| New follows | Lower | 30-40% | Thank + engage |

**Daily routine (10 min):** Check profile views → Review engagement → Check followers → Prioritize and reach out.

#### Tier 2 — Alpha Signals (Brendan Short)

Unique to SlideHeroes, not available to competitors.

| Signal | Detection Method | Action |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Product signup, no first presentation | BigQuery | Nurture DM sequence |
| Presentation created, not shared/exported | BigQuery | DM offering help |
| Course customer hasn't migrated to platform | BigQuery | Re-engagement DM |
| Website pricing page visit (ICP match) | RB2B (when implemented) | Connection request + note |
| Marketing email engagement (last 30 days) | Loops/BigQuery | Warm outreach |

#### Tier 3 — Niche Signals

Require external monitoring but are high-value for our ICP.

| Signal | Detection Method | Action |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Consulting firm hiring for BD/proposals roles | Sales Navigator + Clay | Connection request referencing the role |
| "Pitch deck" or "presentation" in job descriptions | Clay / job board scraping | Connection request with relevant insight |
| Consulting firm won new contract | News alerts | Congratulate + offer help with pitch materials |
| New consulting firm founded | Sales Navigator alerts | Connection request, founder-to-founder |
| Partner/director promotion at consultancy | Sales Navigator | Congratulate + relevant content |

#### Tier 4 — Commodity Signals (Use Sparingly)

Everyone has these. Never use alone.

- Generic job changes
- Funding rounds
- Website visits (non-ICP)
- Generic intent data

### 4. Connection & DM Strategy

#### Connection Requests

**Rule:** Always include a note. Generic connection requests signal laziness.

**Framework** (McKenna SMYKM):
1. Research 3-5 specific details about the person
2. Reference something specific in the note
3. Make it about them, not you

**Example:**
> "Hi [Name], saw your post about [specific topic]. We're solving a similar challenge at SlideHeroes — helping consultancies build better pitch decks faster. Would love to connect."

#### DM Sequences — Permission-Based Selling (Anton Maker)

**The PBS Framework:**
1. **Ask for permission** — "Would you be open to hearing an idea?"
2. **Get the first yes fast** — "Can I share one observation?" (low commitment)
3. **Build momentum** — Each yes leads to the next. Never skip steps.
4. **If they say no, stop.** No guilt trips, no persistence.

#### DM Templates

**Profile View Follow-Up:**
> "Hey [Name], noticed you stopped by my profile. I see you're leading [function] at [Company]. We work with a lot of [similar roles] at [similar company type] on [specific problem]. Curious — what brought you to my profile?"

**Post Comment Follow-Up:**
> "Hey [Name], really appreciate your comment on my [topic] post. Your point about [specific thing they said] got me thinking. We worked with a company facing that exact challenge — [one sentence]. I wrote a deeper breakdown of how they solved it. Want me to send it over?"

**Engagement-Triggered Outreach:**
> "Hey [Name], thanks for engaging with my content on [topic]. I noticed you're at [Company] — we've been working with similar firms on [specific problem]. Happy to share what's working if useful. No pitch, just insights."

#### CTA Rules (Brendan Short + McKenna)

**Never ask for time in first message:**
- ❌ "Worth 15 minutes?"
- ❌ "Open to a quick chat?"
- ❌ "Would you be interested in a demo?"
- ❌ Calendar links (EVER in first contact — McKenna)

**Give value instead:**
- ✅ Share an insight about their market
- ✅ Offer a relevant resource or framework
- ✅ Send a case study of a similar firm
- ✅ Offer a free presentation audit

### 5. Engagement Strategy

**"Help people publicly"** — Alex Boyd

Spending time engaging with prospects' content is as valuable as creating your own.

**Daily engagement routine:**
1. Comment thoughtfully on 5-10 target prospects' posts
2. Share relevant posts with your perspective added
3. Respond to every comment on your own posts
4. Check and act on trigger signals (profile views, likes, follows)

**Comment strategy:**
- Add genuine insight, not "Great post!"
- Ask a thoughtful follow-up question
- Share a relevant experience
- Respectfully offer a different perspective

### 6. Metrics — Leads, Not Engagement

**"Engagement and leads are two different games."** — Anton Maker

| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| **Profile views/week** | Visibility | Trending up |
| **Connection acceptance rate** | Relevance of targeting | 40%+ |
| **DM response rate** | Quality of messaging | 30%+ |
| **Conversations started/week** | Pipeline activity | 5-10 |
| **Meetings booked/month** | Business outcome | 4-8 |
| **Signal → conversation rate** | Signal quality | 15%+ |

**Do NOT optimize for:**
- Follower count
- Post impressions
- Like count
- Viral posts

## AI Copy Tells to Avoid

The bar rises every few months. Current tells to avoid in ALL outreach (Brendan Short):

| Tell | Why It's Bad |
|------|-------------|
| "Was reading..." | No one says this naturally |
| "That's exactly..." | Obviously AI-assembled |
| "Curious if your team's explored..." | Robotic phrasing |
| "Noticed..." | Becoming a tell (was fine in 2024) |
| M-dashes (—) overuse | AI signature |
| Overly structured sentences | Feels assembled, not written |
| "I'd love to..." | Classic AI filler |
