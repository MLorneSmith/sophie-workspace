Kanban Tasks

Phase 1: The Start

A. Identify WHO you audience is

1. Determine who the hero of your presentation is (hint it is not you - it is your audience)
2. Profile your Audience using an Audience Map
3. Identify your audience's pain points and montivations

B. Determine WHY you are speaking with them

1. Determine the Context of the Presentation
2. Identify the Catalyst of the Presentation or meeting
3. Determine the central Question you aiduence wants answered
4. Determine the Objective / Next Step for the Meeting
5. Plan the Action Agenda for the first 72 hours

C. Determine WHAT your answer is to your audience's quest

1. Gather existing work and improve it
2. Develop new thinking
Run a brainstorming session
3. Summarize and organize your ideas

D. Build an arguement map to define the logic of your Answer

1. Build an inductive argument
2. Structure evidence into MECE groups
3. Develop the 'so what?' for each point
4. Focus on logical levels of abstraction
5. Identify potential objections and questions

Phase 2: The Art of Storytelling

A. Develop a text-based outline of the presentation

1. Develop your Introduction with your Context, Catalyst and Question
2. Convert your argument map to a set of bullets that Answer your audiences Question
3. Focus on main 'headline' messages as main bullets
4. Create supporting ideas as sub-bullets under your main bullets

B. Identify where to potentially inject stories

1. Design stories that stick
2. Test story resonance with a sample audience

C. Storyboard the presentation

1. Convert the outline into a storyboard
2. Start to think about how to visually communicate your ideas

Phase 3: The Harmony of Design

A. Develop a Slide Template

1. Use ours
2. Create a color palette
3. Select custom fonts
4. Design title slide and section dividers
5. Build an icon and image library

B. Design each slide

1. Write out each heading for each slide
2. Write out each sub-heading for each supporting piece of evidence/chart
3. Design charts and supporting visuals
4. Open PowerPoint!
5. Populate slides with supporting evidence
6. Review slides for design principle violations

Phase 4: The Science of Fact-based Persuasion

A. Develop Graphs and Tables for your data

1. Determine what tables or chart types are best for the data you wish to show
2. Create the tables or charts
3. Reduce and eliminate chart-junk
4. Design tables and graphs to emphasize the key data elements that support your story
5. Add source citations to data

Phase 5: The How

A. Review

1. Conduct peer review of content
2. Complete legal and compliance review
3. Conduct final proofread
4. Incorporate feedback and finalize

B. Practice

1. Do an initial run through of the presentation. Speak the presentation out loud and improvise
2. Write this version down as a formal script
3. Run through the presentation two or three more times working on length, simplifying language
4. If the length needs editing, revise the presentation, eliminating or combining slide ideas
5. Present to someone else to solicit feedback and simulate a ‘live’ presentation
6. Run through the script a few more times and then park it
7. Get a good night’s sleep; review the script once or twice just before the presentation

C. Perform

1. Conduct a pre-presentation flight-check to ensure you have everything you need
2. Deliver with conviction, passion and drama
3. Focus on managing stress
4. Answer the Audience's questions

D. Follow-up

1. Execute the Action Agenda
2. Send follow-up materials
3. Conduct personal retrospective
4. Archive presentation materials
