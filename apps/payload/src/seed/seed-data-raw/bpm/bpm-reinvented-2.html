I have a confession to make…

When I first started... I was no different than anyone else. My decks (presentations) stunk.

They were awful. LOL!

When I finally said enough is enough and started getting serious about turning my presentation handicap into a MAJOR professional advantage I initially turned to books and other training materials.

I was shocked at how LITTLE material is oriented towards the types of business presentations I needed to create. 

Sit-down business discussions. 

Not TED-style speeches.

Learning to create a presentation is not the same as learning to give a presentation.

Advice on giving auditorium-style speeches emphasizes the use of imagery and simple, easy to read slides.

When the setting changes, when the audience changes, and you are creating a document for a board meeting, or to update your boss’s boss – the communication requirements change as well. (No pictures of cats please!)

‘Sit-down’ business presentations require a completely different approach to presentation design. 

The focus shifts to the crafting of a tight story:

  • The presentation takes you step by step through an argument
  • There is a clear, logical structure that is sound (bullet proof!)
  • The argument is complete
  • The presentation is data intensive, with assertions proven with facts

Excellence comes from mastery of a SET of skills:

  • Ordering ideas in a logical and structured way
  • Utilizing words succinctly, powerfully, accurately
  • Dealing with numbers and communicating data effectively
  • Creating aesthetically pleasing design
  • Commanding a room with charisma and executive presence

Only some of these skills are taught in schools or formally in organizations. Many from this list are either very challenging to master, or are seen (by many) as simply innate.

‘How to use PowerPoint’ courses do not teach these skills either.
