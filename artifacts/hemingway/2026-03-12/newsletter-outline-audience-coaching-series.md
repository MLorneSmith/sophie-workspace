# Newsletter Series Outline: Building Your Audience → Introducing Coaching

## Series Concept

A 6-email newsletter series that does two things simultaneously:
1. **Delivers genuine value** on presentation strategy (builds trust, grows audience)
2. **Naturally introduces SlideHeroes' coaching/consulting offering** without a hard sell

The series follows a "teach → reveal the gap → offer the bridge" arc. Each email stands alone as valuable content, but together they build toward a coaching offer that feels earned, not forced.

## Target Persona

Independent consultants and small consultancy team leads who:
- Create 5-15 client presentations per month
- Know their decks could be stronger but don't have time to level up
- Have tried AI tools but found the output generic
- Value structured thinking (Pyramid Principle, SCQA) but struggle to apply it consistently

## Series Arc

### Email 1: "The Presentation Your Client Actually Remembers"
- **Hook:** Most consulting presentations are forgotten 48 hours after the meeting. Here's why.
- **Core idea:** The difference between a deck that informs and a deck that persuades is structure, not design.
- **Takeaway:** The "One Slide Test" — if your recommendation doesn't fit on one slide with a clear so-what, the thinking isn't done.
- **CTA:** Reply and tell me: what's the hardest presentation you've had to build this year?

### Email 2: "Why AI-Generated Slides Feel Wrong (And What to Do Instead)"
- **Hook:** You've tried Gamma, Beautiful.ai, Tome. The slides look fine. The thinking is hollow.
- **Core idea:** AI tools optimize for visual output, not narrative structure. They skip the hardest part: deciding what to say and in what order.
- **Takeaway:** A 3-step pre-AI process that makes AI-generated slides actually useful.
- **CTA:** Forward this to a colleague who's been burned by AI decks.

### Email 3: "The Framework McKinsey Uses (That Nobody Teaches)"
- **Hook:** Pyramid Principle is mentioned everywhere. Almost nobody applies it correctly.
- **Core idea:** Walk through SCQA (Situation, Complication, Question, Answer) with a real consulting example — before/after.
- **Takeaway:** Template: How to structure your next recommendation deck in 15 minutes using SCQA.
- **CTA:** Download the SCQA one-pager (lead magnet — gated).

### Email 4: "I Watched 200 Consultant Presentations. Here's What Separates Good from Great."
- **Hook:** After two decades at Mastercard, Oliver Wyman, and LBS, Mike has seen the full spectrum. 
- **Core idea:** The 3 patterns of great presentations (clear so-what per slide, evidence hierarchy, deliberate repetition) vs. the 3 patterns of mediocre ones (data dumps, buried insights, "any questions?" endings).
- **Takeaway:** Checklist: 6 questions to ask before presenting to a client.
- **CTA:** How would your last deck score? Reply and I'll give you honest feedback (engagement driver).

### Email 5: "The Bottleneck Isn't Your Slides — It's Your Process"
- **Hook:** You're spending 4 hours on a deck that should take 90 minutes. The problem isn't speed.
- **Core idea:** Most consultants jump to slides before the thinking is done. They iterate in PowerPoint instead of iterating on paper. This is the gap SlideHeroes closes.
- **Takeaway:** The "Napkin First" method — sketch your argument in 5 bullet points before opening any tool.
- **Soft intro:** "We're building something at SlideHeroes that forces this thinking-first approach. More on that soon."

### Email 6: "We're Opening 10 Coaching Spots"
- **Hook:** Direct, no preamble. "For the past 5 emails, I've shared frameworks for better presentations. Now I want to work with you directly."
- **Core idea:** Introduce SlideHeroes coaching — a structured engagement where Mike personally reviews your presentation process and helps you build a repeatable system.
- **Offer details:** What's included, who it's for, what results look like, limited spots (scarcity, but real).
- **CTA:** Apply for a coaching spot (link to application form or Calendly).

## Cadence

- **Frequency:** Weekly (Tuesday or Wednesday, 10am ET)
- **Total run:** 6 weeks
- **Entry point:** Email 1 sent to full list; also usable as a welcome sequence for new subscribers

## Metrics to Track

- Open rates per email (benchmark: 35%+ for niche B2B)
- Reply rates (Emails 1 and 4 designed to drive replies)
- Lead magnet downloads (Email 3)
- Coaching applications (Email 6)
- Unsubscribe rate (should stay under 1% if value is genuine)
