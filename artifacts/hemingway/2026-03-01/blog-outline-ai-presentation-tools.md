# Outline: AI Presentation Tools Compared

## Core Idea
Every AI presentation tool makes your slides faster. Almost none of them make your slides *better*. Here's what actually works in 2026 — and where the market still fails consultants.

## Target Keyword
"AI presentation tools"

## Audience
Individual consultants and small/medium consultancies evaluating AI tools for client-facing presentations.

## Structure

### 1. Hook / Opening (~200 words)
- Open with the consultant's real problem: you don't need faster slides, you need better thinking
- Every tool promises "presentations in seconds" — but seconds of what?
- Set up the honest comparison frame: tested 8 tools, here's what actually matters

### 2. The Three Tribes of AI Presentation Tools (~300 words)
- AI-native standalone (Gamma, Tome, Beautiful.ai, Slidebean, Pitch)
- Legacy suite bolt-ons (Microsoft Copilot, Google Gemini)
- Design platforms with AI sprinkled in (Canva)
- Why the category matters for how you evaluate them

### 3. Tool-by-Tool Verdicts (~1,200 words)
Quick, opinionated takes on each tool — what it's good for, what it's not, who should use it:
- **Gamma** — The market leader. Fast, flexible, impressive for first drafts. But the AI writes like it's filling a template, not building an argument.
- **Beautiful.ai** — Best design guardrails. Enterprise-ready. But no free tier and team pricing gets steep.
- **Microsoft Copilot** — The enterprise play. Context-aware via Graph. But $42-87/user/month is brutal, and you're locked in.
- **Google Slides + Gemini** — If you're already in Workspace, it's fine. Just "fine."
- **Pitch** — Best collaboration. Agency-grade. Credit system is annoying.
- **Canva AI** — Jack of all trades, master of none for presentations. Price hike backlash.
- **Tome** — Pivoting away from presentations. Interesting narrative format but risky bet.
- **Slidebean** — Niche startup/pitch deck tool. Good for fundraising, limited elsewhere.

### 4. The Gap Nobody's Filling (~300 words)
- Every tool optimizes for speed and aesthetics
- None enforce structured thinking (Pyramid Principle, SCQA, MECE)
- Consultants need structure > decoration
- The difference between "looks professional" and "is professional"
- Where SlideHeroes fits: framework-native AI that thinks before it designs

### 5. Practical Recommendations (~300 words)
Decision matrix by use case:
- Quick internal decks → Gamma
- Enterprise teams already on M365 → Copilot
- Startup pitch decks → Slidebean
- Agency/sales teams → Pitch
- Consulting-grade client work → SlideHeroes (coming soon)
- General design work → Canva
- Google Workspace shops → Gemini in Slides

### 6. Closing (~200 words)
- The real question isn't which AI tool makes slides fastest
- It's which one helps you think clearly — because your client is paying for your thinking, not your formatting
- CTA: Join SlideHeroes waitlist / newsletter for consulting-grade AI presentations
