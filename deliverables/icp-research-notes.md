# ICP Research Notes — Best Practices for B2B SaaS Startups

*Compiled 2026-02-12 for SlideHeroes ICP definition (Task #268)*

---

## Key Frameworks Reviewed

### 1. Lincoln Murphy (Sixteen Ventures) — Situational ICP Framework
- **Core insight:** ICP is NOT universal or permanent — it's situational (time-bound, goal-specific, capability-matched)
- **Key principle:** "You get to choose your customers. If you don't, they choose you — and they may be less-than-ideal."
- ICP comes BEFORE persona development — you can't define personas until you know what type of company you're targeting
- Overcome FOMO: focusing on one ICP doesn't mean excluding everyone forever — it means dedicating resources for a defined period (3-6 months)
- **ICP dimensions:** Ready (have the problem now), Willing (motivated to solve it), Able (budget + authority to buy)
- Churn is often a symptom of acquiring non-ideal customers

### 2. Lenny Rachitsky — B2B ICP Identification
- **Core insight:** Most founders initially got their ICP wrong. Everyone landed on at least 3 attributes.
- Pick the 3 most unique/important characteristics: company size, job title, pain point, way of working, tech used, type of business
- **Four signs you're getting closer to your ICP:**
  1. Significant increase in conversion rate
  2. Significant increase in enthusiasm
  3. Stronger desire to take action now
  4. "The nod" — visceral recognition of the problem
- Data from outbound sales is the best signal (vs. investor/friend intros)
- Quote from Mathilde Collin (Front): "We did not think about ICP. I wish we did earlier on. It's one of my biggest mistakes."

### 3. MRR Unlocked Framework (Alex Estner)
- **Two-part ICP:** Ideal Company + Ideal Personas (Buyer + User)
- **Company dimensions:** Size, stage/type, company-specific criteria
- **Persona dimensions:** Job titles, department, team size, education, interests
- **Deep dive:** Daily activities, responsibilities, KPIs, jobs to be done, pain points
- **Behavioral layer:** Software tools used, purchasing process, complementary products, content consumed
- **Buying signals/trigger events:** Observable indicators that suggest readiness to buy
- **Anti-ICP:** Equally important to define who is NOT your customer
- **6 ways to build ICP:**
  - With customers: CRM analysis (won vs lost), product analytics (engagement, churn)
  - Without customers: Dream 100 list, market testing (A/B outbound), community research, sales call feedback
- **ICP validation indicators:** Improved conversion rates, great customer feedback, early referrals/virality

### 4. Cornel Lazar — 5-Phase ICP Framework for SaaS
- **Phase 1: Spot Success Signals** — Commercial health, product usage, strategic fit, customer success
- **Phase 2: Build Data Foundation** — Quantitative (CRM, analytics), qualitative (interviews), scrappy (founder notes, LinkedIn)
- **Phase 3: Broad Filters** — Industry, company size, geography, tech stack (but firmographics alone are weak predictors!)
- **Phase 4: Hidden Insights** — Vertical-specific metrics, product usage patterns, user profiles, adoption barriers
- **Phase 5: Confirm & Prioritize** — Win rates, ACV, adoption, satisfaction across segments
- **Key insight:** ICP ≠ Persona ≠ Segment. Quick test: "If you can't describe your ICP without mentioning personas, you haven't defined it properly."
- Map personas to buying funnel (end users = TOFU, decision-makers = BOFU)

### 5. Gartner — ICP Development Framework
- ICP focused on "most valuable customers that are also most likely to buy"
- Distinguishes ICP from broader "target customer" concept

## Common Dimensions to Include

| Category | Attributes |
|----------|-----------|
| **Firmographics** | Company size (employees/revenue), industry/vertical, stage (startup/growth/enterprise), geography, business model |
| **Technographics** | Current tools, tech sophistication, integration requirements |
| **Pain Points** | Specific problems, urgency, current workarounds, cost of status quo |
| **Buying Signals** | Trigger events, budget availability, timeline, decision process |
| **Behavioral** | Content consumed, communities, conferences, purchasing patterns |
| **Success Potential** | Likelihood of achieving value, expansion potential, reference value |

## Common Mistakes

1. **Too broad / no focus** — trying to serve everyone, serving no one well
2. **Confusing ICP with persona** — ICP = company type, persona = person within company
3. **Relying only on firmographics** — industry + size alone are weak predictors
4. **Not defining anti-personas** — knowing who NOT to pursue is equally valuable
5. **Setting ICP once and never revising** — should be living document, revisited every 3-6 months
6. **FOMO-driven expansion** — fear of missing out leads to premature broadening
7. **Using investor/friend intros as signal** — biased sample; outbound data is better
8. **Skipping validation** — treating ICP as theoretical vs. testing with real conversations

---

## Pre-Revenue ICP Validation Methods

1. **Dream 100 List** — Identify 100 ideal companies, find patterns in their attributes
2. **Outbound A/B Testing** — Test different ICP hypotheses with cold outreach campaigns
3. **Community Research** — Find where target customers discuss their pain points
4. **Sales Conversations** — Use "The Mom Test" approach for honest customer discovery
5. **Competitor Analysis** — Study who competitors' best customers are (case studies, reviews)
6. **LinkedIn Research** — Profile ideal buyers, study their content and engagement
7. **Founder Network** — Leverage Mike's consulting background for warm intros
8. **Landing Page Tests** — Different value props for different segments, measure conversion
