## Part 1: Subject Lines That Work

### Personalized / Research-Based
```
Quick question about {{companyName}}'s growth plans
{{firstName}}, question about {{specificTopic}}
Thoughts on {{companyName}}'s {{specificInitiative}}
A thought on {{companyName}}'s campaign tracking
Quick question about {{companyName}}'s blog
```

### Mutual Connection
```
{{mutualConnection}} suggested I reach out
Introduction from {{mutualConnection}}
A question from a friend of {{mutualConnection}}
```

### Trigger Event
```
Congrats on {{triggerEvent}} - quick thought
Saw the news about {{companyName}}
Your new role at {{companyName}}
```

### Problem-Focused
```
{{firstName}}, struggling with {{painPoint}}?
The key to dealing with {{problem}}?
Tired of messy campaign data?
```

### Competitor-Focused
```
{{firstName}}, quick question about {{competitorName}}
Noticed you're using {{competitorName}}
{{competitorName}} saw a 25% lift
```

### Direct / Short
```
15 mins - {{valueProposition}}?
Quick question, {{firstName}}
This might interest you
Idea for {{companyName}}
{{firstName}} - wrong person?
```

### Curiosity Gap
```
Your competitor {{competitorName}}
A thought on your content strategy
Broken link on your site
```

**Subject Line Rules:**
- Under 50 characters
- No ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation
- Avoid spam triggers: "FREE", "Act now", "Limited time"
- Topic over pitch — describe what it's about, not what you want
- Test relentlessly

## Part 2: Opening Lines (Hooks)

### Research-Based Openers
```
Saw your post about {{specificTopic}} — the point about {{specificInsight}} really resonated with me...

Your team's expansion into {{market}} is impressive — especially how you approached {{specificChallenge}}...

I noticed on your LinkedIn that you're managing multiple marketing channels for {{companyName}}.

I was looking at your blog post, "{{blogTitle}}," and noticed you're currently ranking #8 for the keyword "{{targetKeyword}}."

Saw you're hiring reps.
```

### Trigger Event Openers
```
Congrats on the move to {{companyName}}!

I saw that you're scaling the sales team at {{companyName}} and thought this might be relevant.

Given your role at {{companyName}}, I imagine staying ahead of evolving {{industry}} regulations is a constant priority.
```

### Mutual Connection Openers
```
{{mutualConnection}} and I were discussing the challenges of scaling engineering teams, and she mentioned the incredible growth you're overseeing at {{companyName}}.

If you remember {{sharedExperience}}...
```

### Pattern Interrupt Openers
```
Are you interested in knowing more about how you could {{desiredOutcome}}?

We have been registering a marked increase in downloads from our website, including yours.

It has come to my attention that your company has been dealing with {{specificProblem}}...
```

**Opening Line Rules:**
- Never start with "I hope this finds you well"
- Never start with "My name is..."
- Reference something specific (not name-drop)
- Lead with them, not you

```
Would you be open to a brief 15-minute call next week to see if we can help you get a clearer picture of your marketing efforts?

Best,
{{yourName}}
```

## Part 4: Follow-Up Sequences

### Timing Guidelines
- **Wait 3-5 days** between each follow-up
- **Max 3-4 follow-ups** total
- Add new value in each follow-up
- Change approach after 2-3 attempts

### Follow-Up 1: The Thoughtful Bump (3-5 days later)
```
Subject: Re: {{originalSubject}}

Hey {{firstName}},

Given that you're growing the sales team, I thought this would be worth discussing.

Did you have any feedback on my note?

{{yourName}}
```

### Follow-Up 2: The Clarification (3-5 days later)
```
Subject: Re: {{originalSubject}}

Hey {{firstName}},

Given you're growing the sales team, I thought coaching might be top of mind.

To clarify, we've built a {{briefProductDescription}}. Sits right inside their {{workflow}}.

High-growth teams like {{socialProof}} get great results.

Think this could help your team?

{{yourName}}
```

### Follow-Up 3: Neutral Insight (3-5 days later)
```
Subject: Re: {{originalSubject}}

{{firstName}}, do you read {{industryBlog}}'s blog?

Given you're likely ramping reps, I thought you'd find this interesting.

The VP of Sales Dev at {{exampleCompany}} wrote about how she scaled her team to a {{impressiveOutcome}}.

Check it out: {{link}}

{{yourName}}

P.S. Any thoughts on my last note?
```

### Follow-Up 4: The Breakup (Final email)
```
Subject: {{firstName}} - wrong person?

Hey {{firstName}},

I've reached out a few times because you're {{reasonForOutreach}}.

I thought {{yourSolution}} could help.

I'm going to chalk it up to my timing being off. Let me know if I'm wrong, but I'll stop my outreach for now.

Do you think this will be a priority in the next two quarters?

{{yourName}}
```

## Part 5: Call-to-Action Examples

### Low-Friction CTAs (Highest Response)
```
Worth a quick chat?

Worth exploring?

Would this be helpful?

Does this resonate?

Am I off base here?

Interested in comparing notes?
```

### Specific Time CTAs
```
Would you be open to a 15-minute call next week?

Would Tuesday or Thursday work for a quick chat?

15 mins to discuss?
```

### Permission CTAs
```
If you're open to it, I'd love to share more.

Mind if I send over a quick case study?

Would it be helpful to see how {{similarCompany}} approached this?
```

### Binary Question CTAs
```
Is improving {{metric}} a priority this quarter?

Is {{challenge}} something your team is focused on?

Think this could help your team?
```

**CTA Rules:**
- One CTA per email (never multiple asks)
- Make it easy to say yes
- Make it easy to say no ("If not, no worries")
- Binary questions outperform open-ended

## Part 7: What NOT to Do

### Spam Trigger Words to Avoid
- FREE, FREE!!!
- Act now
- Limited time
- Guaranteed
- No obligation
- Click here
- Urgent
- Winner

### Phrases to Never Use
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "My name is..."
- "I'm reaching out because..."
- "Just checking in"
- "Per my last email"
- "Touch base"
- "Circle back"
- "Synergy"

### Structural Mistakes
- Emails over 150 words
- More than one CTA
- HTML-heavy formatting
- Big paragraphs (keep to 1-2 lines)
- Leading with your product/company story
- Fake urgency or manufactured scarcity
- Following up 5x in one week
