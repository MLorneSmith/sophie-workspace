## The Idea

User dumps everything in their head — unstructured, messy, stream of consciousness — and SlideHeroes transforms it into a structured SCQA outline with a complete slide plan. In seconds.

This is an **alternative entry point** to the guided Assemble flow. Users choose how they want to start:

```
How do you want to start?

  📝 Brain Dump     → Paste your messy thoughts, we'll organize them
  🎯 Guided         → Step-by-step SCQA builder (Assemble flow)
  📄 Upload         → Start from an existing deck (WOW #2)
```

All three paths converge at the Outline step — same structured output, different starting points.

---

## Why This Is a WOW

1. **The transformation is the magic.** Chaos in → clarity out. That's what consultants do for their clients. SlideHeroes does it for the consultants.

2. **Speed.** Brain dump in 30 seconds of typing → structured outline in 5 seconds of processing. The near-instant transformation is the wow moment.

3. **Teaches the SlideHeroes way.** Users learn SCQA and pyramid principle by seeing their messy thoughts organized into a proven framework. They get *better at presenting* by using the tool.

4. **Builds trust in the workflow.** "The AI understood my mess and organized it — I trust it with the next steps too." This sells the full Profile → Assemble → Outline → Storyboard → Generate pipeline.

5. **User stays in control.** They get an outline, not a finished deck. They can reorder, add, remove, refine. Ownership = satisfaction.

6. **Sells the product philosophy.** AI + structured workflow > AI alone. The outline (not the finished deck) is the deliverable because the workflow matters.

## How It Works

### Input
A freeform text box. No structure required. Examples:

**Short brain dump:**
> "Need to pitch Deloitte on a partnership. They want AI tools for their SMB practice. We're better than Tome. $50/seat pricing. Q2 pilot."

**Long brain dump:**
> "We need to pitch Deloitte on a partnership. They're expanding their SMB practice and looking at AI tools to improve consultant productivity. Main competitors are Tome and Beautiful.ai but neither does consulting-grade structure. Pricing would be per-seat, maybe $50/user/month. The sponsor is their SVP of Innovation, she's been evaluating AI tools for 6 months. Key risk is they'll want to build in-house. Timeline is Q2 for a pilot. We should probably address the build-vs-buy question head on. Also need to show ROI — if each consultant saves 5 hours/week on decks that's huge."

**Even messier:**
> "deloitte partnership pitch. smb practice growing. they need ai for presentations. we're the only consulting-grade tool. tome and beautiful.ai are competitors but they're generic. $50/seat. sarah chen is the sponsor, svp innovation. she's been looking for 6mo. risk: NIH syndrome. q2 pilot. roi = 5hrs/week/consultant saved"

### Processing

1. **Extract key elements:** topic, audience, context, goals, constraints, risks, data points
2. **Identify presentation type:** pitch, proposal, strategy review, board update, etc.
3. **Build SCQA framework:** organize the chaos into Situation → Complication → Question → Answer
4. **Generate slide outline:** logical flow, consulting-grade structure, appropriate depth

### Output: Structured Outline

**SCQA Framework:**

> **Situation:** Deloitte is expanding its SMB advisory practice and seeking AI tools to improve consultant productivity and output quality
>
> **Complication:** Current presentation tools (Tome, Beautiful.ai) lack consulting-grade narrative structure. Building in-house would take 12+ months and divert engineering resources from core practice
>
> **Question:** How can Deloitte accelerate SMB consultant output with a purpose-built AI presentation platform?
>
> **Answer:** SlideHeroes partnership — per-seat deployment at $50/user/month, with a Q2 pilot proving 5+ hours/week saved per consultant

**Slide Outline:**

1. Executive Summary & Recommendation
2. Deloitte SMB Practice — Growth Trajectory & Opportunity
3. The Consultant Productivity Gap (time spent on decks)
4. Why Current Tools Fall Short (Tome / Beautiful.ai comparison)
5. Introducing SlideHeroes — Built for Consulting
6. How It Works (4-step workflow demo)
7. ROI Model — 5 Hours/Week × Consultant Headcount
8. Partnership Model & Per-Seat Pricing
9. Q2 Pilot Plan — Scope, Timeline, Success Metrics
10. Build vs. Buy Analysis (addressing the key risk)
11. Next Steps & Ask

*Each slide shows a one-line takeaway + suggested content notes*

### Then: User Edits → Continues Workflow

The outline is fully editable. User can:
- Reorder slides
- Add or remove slides
- Edit the SCQA framing
- Adjust slide takeaways
- Then proceed to Storyboard → Generate

## Alternative Entry Points — Full Picture

| Entry Point | What User Provides | What They Get | Best For |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------|----------|
| **Brain Dump** (WOW #3) | Messy thoughts, stream of consciousness | Structured SCQA + slide outline | Users who know what they want to say but not how to structure it |
| **Guided Assemble** | Step-by-step answers (topic, audience, type, key messages) | Structured SCQA + slide outline | Users who want hand-holding or are new to consulting structure |
| **Upload** (WOW #2) | Existing PowerPoint file | Rewritten deck + extracted template | Users with existing content to improve |

All three converge at the **Outline** step. From there, the workflow is identical: Outline → Storyboard → Generate.

## Open Questions

1. **Input format** — text only, or also voice input? ("Talk through your deck idea")
2. **Length limits** — minimum/maximum for the brain dump? Too short = not enough context. Too long = noise.
3. **Confidence indicators** — should the AI flag where it's guessing? "I assumed this is a sales pitch — correct?"
4. **Iterative refinement** — can the AI ask 1-2 clarifying questions before generating? Or is the magic in zero-question output?
5. **Multiple outlines** — offer 2-3 alternative structures? "Here's a narrative approach vs. a data-led approach"

## Success Metrics

- **Entry point split:** % choosing Brain Dump vs. Guided vs. Upload
- **Outline acceptance rate:** % proceeding to Storyboard without major edits
- **Time to outline:** seconds from brain dump submission to structured output
- **Completion rate:** Brain Dump users who finish a full deck vs. Guided users
