### Day 1-2: Profile Optimization

Do this in order. Don't skip to content before your profile works.

#### Headline

**Current problem (likely):** Job title + company. That's a résumé entry, not a positioning statement.

**Formula:** [Who you help] + [Outcome you deliver] + [Proof/Credibility anchor]

**Your headline (recommended):**
```
Helping consultancies win more pitches with AI-powered presentations | Ex-Mastercard | SlideHeroes
```

Or, test this more conversational version:
```
Ex-Mastercard. 20 years watching consultants lose pitches to bad decks. Now I'm fixing that.
```

The second is riskier but more memorable. Pick one and commit. You can A/B test later.

**Not this:**
- ❌ "CEO at SlideHeroes"
- ❌ "AI Startup Founder | Presentations | Consulting"
- ❌ Anything with "passionate about"

#### Banner

Your banner is a billboard. Use it.

**What to include:**
- One-line value prop ("AI-powered presentations for consulting firms")
- Social proof anchor ("Used by consultancies across [regions/sectors]" or a client logo if you have permission)
- Optional: website URL

**How to make it:** Canva has free LinkedIn banner templates. Keep it clean — dark background, clear text. If you want it done properly, Upwork designer runs $50-100.

#### About Section

**Rule:** Open with your prospect's problem, not your journey. They're skimming to know if you're relevant to them. Make the first 3 lines about their pain.

**Structure:**

```
[Line 1-2: The problem your ICP lives with]
[Line 3-5: Who you are and why you're the one to fix it — credibility markers]
[Line 6-8: What SlideHeroes does, specifically]
[Line 9-10: Social proof — case study stat, firm types you work with]
[Line 11: CTA — what you want them to do next]
```

**Draft to start from:**

> If your consultants are spending 30%+ of engagement time reformatting slides — that's billable hours going into a black hole.
>
> I spent 20 years at Mastercard watching this happen from both sides: as a buyer of consulting work, and as a Mastercard Advisors consultant building decks for clients. The formatting problem never got solved. I left to solve it.
>
> SlideHeroes is AI-powered presentation software built specifically for consulting firms — not for general business use. It understands consulting methodology, McKinsey-style structure, and the specific visual language your clients expect.
>
> We work with boutique and mid-size consultancies who want their teams spending time on insight, not on formatting.
>
> [Connect with me if you're building a consultancy and tired of the slide tax your team pays every engagement.]

Edit this in your voice. The structure is what matters.

#### Featured Section

**Purpose:** Convert profile visitors into leads or qualified connections. Feature your strongest proof.

**What to put here (in order of priority):**
1. A case study post or article ("How [firm type] cut proposal time by X%")
2. A free resource (template, checklist, framework download)
3. A media feature or podcast appearance (if you have one)

**Not this:** Motivational posts, generic company announcements, anything that doesn't answer "why should I trust this person?"

**Rule:** Results and numbers, not responsibilities.

Connect the experience to your current narrative. Every entry should either build authority or tell the story.

### The Content Framework

Every post maps to two dimensions:

**Format Pillar (how you write):**
- **Tactical (40%):** Step-by-step, actionable. "3 things every consulting proposal needs." Easiest to write, most shared.
- **Transformational (20%):** Case study with results. "How [firm type] cut proposal prep from 2 weeks to 3 days." Boyd's curiosity gap lives here.
- **Insightful (20%):** Hot take, contrarian opinion. "Why most consulting pitches fail before the first slide."
- **Personal (20%):** Story, behind-the-scenes. "What 20 years at Mastercard taught me about presentations."

**Theme Pillar (what you write about):**
- Presentation Craft
- Strategic Thinking / Consulting Methodology
- Consulting Business (winning work, pricing, BD)
- Leadership & Communication
- AI & Future of Work
- Founder Journey

**Content Mix — 70-20-10:**
- **70% Problem Awareness:** Make them feel the pain. Don't mention SlideHeroes.
- **20% Solution Education:** Show approaches. Still no product push.
- **10% Product/Service:** Case studies with results and soft mentions.

At 3 posts/week, this means: 2 problem posts, 1 solution/education post per week. Product content is once or twice a month, max.

### Post Formatting Rules (Gio Laga / LinkedIn Algorithm)

These are non-negotiable:

- **First 2 lines are everything.** They show before "see more." If they don't earn the click, the post is dead.
- **Short paragraphs.** 1-2 sentences max. White space is your friend.
- **No links in the post body.** Put links in the first comment — LinkedIn suppresses external links in posts.
- **No hashtag soup.** 0-3 hashtags, at the very end if at all.
- **No emojis unless intentional.** 1-2 max. Not decorative — structural (as bullet points or emphasis).

**Hook test:** Read your first 2 lines out loud. Would a partner at a boutique consultancy, skimming their feed on a Tuesday morning, stop and click "see more"? If not, rewrite the hook.

### The SMYKM Connection Note (McKenna)

Every connection request needs a note. Generic requests signal laziness. You have 300 characters — use them.

**The Research Process (5 min per person):**
1. Read their last 3-5 posts
2. Check their current role and firm
3. Note any mutual connections, shared experiences, or specific content they engaged with
4. Find the one specific thing you'll reference

**The Note Formula:**
```
[Specific reference to what triggered the connection] + [1 sentence on relevance] + [No ask]
```

**Examples for Mike:**

*For someone who commented on your post:*
> "Your point about proposal turnaround time on my deck structure post — that's exactly the dynamic I saw at Mastercard Advisors. Would love to connect and compare notes."

*For someone who viewed your profile:*
> "Noticed you stopped by — you're working on some interesting things at [firm]. Would love to connect with other consultancy leaders thinking about presentation efficiency."

*For someone who posted about pitch deck challenges:*
> "Saw your post about proposal fatigue at your firm. Spent 20 years watching this happen at Mastercard. We're solving it at SlideHeroes — would love to connect."

**What you never say:**
- ❌ "I'd like to connect with you"
- ❌ "I found your profile through LinkedIn search"
- ❌ Anything about SlideHeroes features in the connection note
- ❌ "Would love to learn more about your business"
- ❌ Calendar links (ever, in any first contact — McKenna is emphatic on this)

## Week 4: DM Sequences

**Goal:** Start 5-10 conversations this week. Not "reach outs" — actual back-and-forth exchanges.

**Rule:** Never pitch in first contact. The goal of the first DM is to get a reply, not a meeting.

---

### Who Gets a DM

You only DM people with clear signals. In priority order:

1. People who shared your content
2. People who commented substantively on your posts
3. People who've viewed your profile 2+ times
4. People who've connected and then engaged with your content
5. People who've liked 3+ of your posts (Boyd's threshold)

**Never DM:**
- Someone who just accepted your connection request (give them time)
- Someone who liked one post (too weak a signal)
- Someone you haven't met in any way on LinkedIn yet

---

### The Permission-Based Selling (PBS) Framework — Anton Maker

The framework has one rule: **ask permission before advancing.** Every step requires a yes before you take the next one.

```
You → DM opening (about them, not you)
     ↓
Them → Reply (any engagement)
     ↓
You → Acknowledge + ask for permission to share something
     ↓
Them → Yes / No
     ↓ (if yes)
You → Share the value (insight, resource, case study)
     ↓
Them → Engage with it
     ↓
You → Natural next step (question, offer, or soft CTA)
```

**If they say no at any step: stop.** No follow-up. No "just checking in." No guilt. Stop.

**Boyd's principle:** "Waft the food, don't spear them." Create enough curiosity that they lean toward you. Don't chase.

### What You Never Do in a DM

- ❌ Lead with SlideHeroes features
- ❌ Ask for a call or meeting in the first message
- ❌ Send a calendar link (McKenna: never in first contact)
- ❌ Send a demo request
- ❌ "Would you be interested in..." (sounds like a pitch)
- ❌ Follow up more than twice if they don't reply

**What you do instead of asking for their time:**
- Share a relevant insight about their market
- Offer a case study of a similar firm
- Send a free framework or resource
- Offer a free presentation audit (high-value, low-commitment for them)

### The Conversation Arc

A healthy conversation looks like this:

```
Week 1: Connection or first DM
Week 1-2: Back and forth — establishing context and relevance
Week 2-3: You've shared something of value (case study, resource, observation)
Week 3-4: Natural point where a call makes sense — they're asking questions 
           you can't answer in text
Week 4+: "This might be worth a 20-minute call — I can show you exactly 
           how we did it for [similar firm]. Worth it?"
```

The call ask comes naturally when the conversation makes it obvious. You'll know.

**Do this on the last Friday of every month.** Takes 30 minutes.

### The 300-Day Mindset

Anton Maker's most important principle: **don't expect compound results in 30 days.** LinkedIn is a trust-building platform. The first 90 days feel slow. Months 3-6 feel like you're getting traction. Month 9-12 is where it compounds.

**What this means practically:**
- You're building a content library that keeps working for you
- You're building a reputation that precedes your outreach
- People who aren't ready today will remember you when they are (Boyd had a prospect respond 3 years later)
- Your connection acceptance rate improves as your content volume grows and people recognize your name

**The only way to fail:** stop before the compound effects kick in.

### Signal → Conversation → Meeting Funnel

```
Signals received/week (profile views + engagement + follows)
        ↓  (act on 30-40% — filter to ICP fits)
Outreach actions taken (connection requests + DMs)
        ↓  (target 40%+ acceptance, 30%+ DM reply rate)
Conversations started (back-and-forth DM exchanges)
        ↓  (target 20%+ → meeting)
Meetings booked
```

**At steady state (Month 3+), the math should look like:**
- 30-50 signals/week
- 10-20 outreach actions
- 5-10 conversations started
- 1-3 meetings booked

If your meetings are low, fix the conversation rate first. If your conversations are low, fix the outreach rate or signal quality. If your signals are low, fix the content.

| Like count | A post with 3 likes from consultancy partners > a post with 300 likes from randoms |
| Comment count (total) | Track ICP comments, not total comments |
| Hashtag reach | Irrelevant. Stop optimizing for it. |
