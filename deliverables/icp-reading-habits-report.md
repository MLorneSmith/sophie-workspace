# ICP Reading Habits Research Report
## SlideHeroes Target Audience: Individual Consultants & Small/Medium Consultancies

**Research Date:** February 12, 2026
**Prepared by:** Research Subagent

---

## Executive Summary

The research reveals that individual consultants and small/medium consultancies are sophisticated content consumers who prioritize educational value, authenticity, and practical utility over promotional content. Their reading habits are characterized by:

- **Primary channels:** Email newsletters (highest trust), LinkedIn (daily engagement), podcasts (commute/leisure), and long-form articles
- **Content formats:** Action-oriented long-form content, bite-sized LinkedIn insights, audio content for multitasking, and downloadable templates/tools
- **Consumption patterns:** Three distinct segments with different timing preferences, but consistent desire for educational content that addresses immediate business challenges
- **Discovery mechanisms:** Strong reliance on peer recommendations, LinkedIn algorithm, and trusted newsletters for tool/resource discovery
- **Key insight:** Consultants view content as a trust-building mechanism first, business development second

## Detailed Findings by Channel/Format

### 1. Email Newsletters: The Ultimate Trust Weapon

**Key Finding:** Email newsletters remain the most effective channel for building long-term trust and nurturing consultant relationships.

**Evidence & Data:**
- Email is the third-highest owned media platform B2B marketers use to distribute content (after website and blog)
- High-quality newsletters with 40-50% open rates significantly exceed the industry average of 29.61%
- Newsletters drive 33% of website traffic from loyal readers who stay on average >3 minutes
- One successful consultancy newsletter reports 75% of subscribers coming from LinkedIn cross-promotion

**What Consultants Want in Newsletters:**
- Educational content first, promotional content second
- Consistency in timing and format (builds reader habits)
- Action-oriented insights that help make informed decisions
- Fresh thinking and exploration of new perspectives
- Authentic content that demonstrates practical expertise

**Notable Consultant-Focused Newsletters:**
- **The Authority** by Luk Smeyers (The Visible Authority) - bi-weekly, focuses on boutique consultancy performance
- **Lara Hogan's Newsletter** - management and leadership insights for tech consultants
- **Consulting Success Newsletter** - weekly content on building profitable consulting businesses
- Various Substack newsletters emerging in the consulting space

### 2. LinkedIn: The Daily Hub

**Key Finding:** LinkedIn is the primary daily content consumption platform, serving as a "live conference room" for consultants to engage with industry conversations.

**Evidence & Data:**
- 95% of consultancy owners have minimal or no LinkedIn presence (major opportunity)
- LinkedIn is used to source feedback, engage with others' posts, and incorporate feedback into content strategy
- LinkedIn posts from thought leaders drive newsletter subscriptions (75% of one newsletter's subscribers)

**Three Distinct Content Consumption Segments (LinkedIn Research):**

**Segment 1: On the Clock (~40% of respondents)**
- Wait until work to read professionally-relevant content
- Most likely to read before, during, and after lunch
- Least likely to read in the evening or on weekends
- Typically commute by car (no commute reading time)

**Segment 2: Off the Clock (~40% of respondents)**
- Do not read during work hours (due to restrictions or lack of access)
- Most likely to read in the morning, at lunch, in the evening, near bedtime, and on weekends
- Highly engaged in evenings and weekends for professional content
- Common in regulated industries (finance, healthcare)

**Segment 3: Around the Clock (~20% of respondents)**
- Read throughout the day and on weekends
- 76% take public transportation to work (median commute: 40 minutes)
- Mobile access is critical for this segment
- Enjoy 40+ hours of reading time per quarter during commutes

**Senior Leaders (Directors, VPs, CXOs) - Key Insights:**
- 1.5x more likely to read in the morning before work (32% vs 22%)
- 1.9x more likely to read during vacation (14% vs 8%)
- Less likely to read during lunch (38% vs 48%)

**Best Times to Publish on LinkedIn:**
- First thing in the morning (popular for checking)
- Lunchtime
- For senior leaders: early morning before work
- For "Off the Clock" segment: evening and weekends

### 4. Long-Form Content: Articles & Blog Posts

**Key Finding:** Long-form, educational content is critical for building authority and demonstrating deep expertise.

**What Consultants Read:**
- Action-oriented content that helps make informed decisions (51% want this)
- Content showing fresh thinking and new perspectives (50% want this)
- Authentic content that puts expertise into practice (48% want this)
- Content addressing their most pressing pain points
- Case studies showing how problems were solved

**Content Marketing Institute Research:**
- 73% of executives say they will look at competitors if their advisory company isn't producing thought leadership content
- 60% of executives report increased demand for thought leadership content
- 50% attest to the critical importance of thought leadership in selecting an advisor

**Content Preferences:**
- Educational value over promotional content
- Problem-resolution advice
- Deep expertise demonstrated through writing
- Data-backed insights and research
- Personal anecdotes and case studies

**Where Consultants Publish/Read:**
- Company blogs (primary owned content channel)
- LinkedIn Articles
- Medium
- Substack (growing popularity)
- Guest posts on industry publications

### 5. Short-Form & Interactive Content

**Key Finding:** Consultants value bite-sized content for quick value extraction and engagement.

**Preferred Formats:**
- LinkedIn carousels and posts with actionable tips
- Short-form video tutorials (YouTube, LinkedIn Video)
- Interactive assessments and quizzes
- Downloadable templates and checklists
- Bite-sized summaries of long-form content

**Content Marketing Trends 2025:**
- People are done with passive scrolling—they want interactive content that adds value
- Gamified and interactive content (quizzes, polls, assessments) gaining traction
- Video content, carousels, and LinkedIn newsletters are key engagement formats

### 6. Video Content

**Key Finding:** Video is growing but secondary to text/audio for professional learning.

**YouTube Channels Consultants Follow:**
- McKinsey & Company (podcasts and insights)
- Management Consulted (interviews, case studies)
- Various consultant educators and thought leaders

**Video Formats That Work:**
- Tutorial videos (how-to guides)
- Interview-based content
- Webinar recordings
- Short-form tips (LinkedIn Video, TikTok for some demographics)

## Peak Consumption Times & Habits

### Daily Patterns by Segment

**On the Clock Segment (40%):**
- Primary reading: Work hours (before, during, after lunch)
- Peak times: Morning start, lunch break, mid-afternoon
- Low reading: Commute, evenings, weekends

**Off the Clock Segment (40%):**
- Primary reading: Morning, lunch, evening, bedtime, weekends
- Peak times: Early morning (before work), evening near bedtime, weekends
- Low reading: During work hours

**Around the Clock Segment (20%):**
- Primary reading: Throughout the day and weekends
- Peak times: Morning commute (40 min median), evening commute, leisure time
- Access: Mobile-first, public transportation users

### Weekly Patterns

**Senior Leaders:**
- More likely to read early morning before work
- More likely to read during vacation
- Less likely to read during lunch

**General Pattern:**
- Monday morning: Catch-up on weekend content, plan week
- Mid-week: Engagement peaks for content delivered earlier in week
- Friday: Lower engagement, content often saved for weekend reading
- Weekend: Significant reading time for "Off the Clock" segment

### Newsletter Sending Best Practices

**Optimal Times:**
- Early morning (before 8am) - highest engagement for LinkedIn newsletters
- Consistent schedule (e.g., every other Wednesday) builds reader habits
- Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday mornings generally perform best
- Avoid Friday afternoons and weekends for B2B content

**Frequency:**
- Weekly: Standard for most successful newsletters
- Bi-weekly: Works for deeper, more comprehensive content
- Monthly: Requires exceptional value to maintain engagement

## How Consultants Discover New Tools & Resources

### Primary Discovery Channels

**1. Word-of-Mouth (Most Trusted)**
- Peer recommendations from other consultants
- Client recommendations and referrals
- Network referrals through LinkedIn connections
- Word-of-mouth drives an estimated $6 trillion in global consumer spending yearly

**2. LinkedIn Algorithm & Network**
- Content shared by connections they trust
- LinkedIn's recommendation engine
- Following thought leaders who share tool recommendations
- Dark social (WhatsApp, email links from colleagues) - may account for 84% of referral traffic

**3. Articles & Blog Posts**
- Tool review articles (e.g., "Best Tools for Consultants in 2024")
- Thought leaders recommending tools in their content
- Case studies showing how tools solved specific problems

**4. Podcast Mentions**
- Podcast hosts recommending tools they use
- Guest mentions during interviews
- Sponsor mentions on consulting podcasts

**5. Independent Research**
- Google searches for specific problems
- Product comparison sites (Capterra, G2, etc.)
- Tool directories and curated lists

### Discovery Challenges for Consultants

According to research:
- 64.3% of consultants are burnt out from lack of work-life balance
- 57.1% are burnt out from lack of time for focused work
- 64.3% are burnt out from notification and distraction fatigue

**Implication:** Consultants have limited time for research and rely heavily on trusted sources for tool discovery. Word-of-mouth and content from trusted consultants are more effective than cold outreach.

## Recommendations for SlideHeroes Content Distribution Strategy

### 1. Prioritize Email Newsletter

**Why:**
- Email is the "ultimate trust weapon" for consultants
- Higher engagement rates than social media
- Builds long-term relationships and nurtures leads over time
- Consultants expect educational content, not promotional spam

**Strategy:**
- Launch a bi-weekly or weekly newsletter focused on presentation strategy for consultants
- Educational content first: presentation tips, templates, case studies
- Minimal promotional content (at the very end, not prominently featured)
- Cross-promote on LinkedIn to drive subscribers (expect 75% from LinkedIn)
- Include consistent sections to build reader habits
- Send early morning (before 8am) or consistent day/time

### 2. Establish Strong LinkedIn Presence

**Why:**
- LinkedIn is the daily hub for consultants
- 95% of consultancy owners have minimal/no LinkedIn presence (opportunity)
- Cross-promotion drives newsletter subscribers
- Engaged audience for bite-sized content

**Strategy:**
- Publish 2-3 LinkedIn posts per week with presentation tips
- Create a LinkedIn Newsletter (in addition to email newsletter)
- Share blog posts, newsletter issues, and short insights
- Engage with other consultants' posts (build community)
- Use LinkedIn as a "live conference room" for sourcing feedback and engaging
- Target early morning posting for maximum engagement
- Consider evening and weekend content for "Off the Clock" segment

### 3. Leverage Podcasts

**Why:**
- Consultants listen during commutes and leisure
- High engagement with host recommendations
- Builds credibility through association

**Strategy:**
- Secure guest spots on top consulting podcasts (Consulting Success, Craft of Consulting, etc.)
- Sponsor relevant podcasts with host-read endorsements
- Consider launching a SlideHeroes podcast on presentation strategy for consultants
- Target 20-40 minute episodes (fits standard commute length)

### 4. Create Long-Form Educational Content

**Why:**
- 73% of executives will look at competitors if you don't produce thought leadership
- 60% of executives demand thought leadership content
- Demonstrates deep expertise and builds authority

**Strategy:**
- Publish long-form articles on presentation strategy, case studies, and research
- Create downloadable templates and checklists (high-value lead magnets)
- Develop original research on presentation challenges consultants face
- Use data and case studies to back up claims
- Write from experience and share personal anecdotes

### 5. Repurpose Content Across Formats

**Why:**
- Consultants consume content in different formats at different times
- Maximizes ROI on content creation
- Reaches different segments (On the Clock vs. Off the Clock)

**Strategy:**
- Long-form articles → LinkedIn posts, newsletter sections, podcast topics
- Newsletter content → LinkedIn Articles, Medium posts
- Podcast episodes → Blog posts, LinkedIn posts, newsletter summaries
- Templates → LinkedIn carousels, newsletter features, lead magnets
- Case studies → Multiple formats: article, LinkedIn post, podcast episode, newsletter section

### 6. Focus on Action-Oriented, Authentic Content

**Why:**
- 51% want action-oriented content
- 50% want fresh thinking and new perspectives
- 48% want authentic content that puts expertise into practice
- Consultants value educational content over promotional content

**Strategy:**
- Every piece of content should include actionable takeaways
- Share unique perspectives and frameworks
- Be authentic and genuine - avoid generic marketing speak
- Use real examples and case studies from your work
- Address consultants' most pressing presentation challenges

### 7. Build Consistency and Expectations

**Why:**
- Consistent frequency builds reader habits
- Subscribers are more likely to look for, open, and engage with content when they know when to expect it
- Becoming part of their routine is a massive achievement

**Strategy:**
- Establish consistent publication schedule (e.g., newsletter every other Wednesday)
- Maintain consistent sections and format within newsletters
- Use predictable LinkedIn posting schedule
- Deliver on promises - if you say weekly, deliver weekly
- 99% consistency in what subscribers receive creates trust

### 8. Target Different Segments Strategically

**Why:**
- Three distinct segments with different timing preferences
- Senior leaders have different consumption patterns
- Different devices and contexts (work vs. commute vs. home)

**Strategy:**
- **On the Clock segment:** Publish during work hours, focus on desktop-friendly formats
- **Off the Clock segment:** Publish mornings, evenings, weekends; mobile-friendly content
- **Around the Clock segment:** Optimize for mobile consumption during commutes
- **Senior leaders:** Early morning content, vacation-friendly reading, shorter formats
- Use email analytics to understand when your specific audience engages most

### 9. Use AI to Enhance, Not Replace, Content Creation

**Why:**
- AI can streamline research, outlining, and drafting
- True thought leadership requires unique insights and personal experience
- 74% of organizations report AI accelerates data analysis
- Consultants can spot AI-generated content lacking authenticity

**Strategy:**
- Use AI for research, topic ideation, and outlining
- Always infuse content with personal insights, case studies, and expertise
- Use AI to analyze data from surveys or client feedback
- Create unique research and perspectives AI can't replicate
- Never rely solely on AI for content creation

### 10. Leverage Trust-Based Discovery Mechanisms

**Why:**
- Word-of-mouth is the most trusted discovery channel
- Dark social (email, WhatsApp) accounts for 84% of referral traffic
- Consultants trust recommendations from peers and trusted sources

**Strategy:**
- Encourage client referrals and testimonials
- Make it easy for satisfied users to share SlideHeroes with their network
- Partner with consulting coaches and thought leaders for recommendations
- Feature testimonials from other consultants prominently
- Create a referral program with incentives

## Content Calendar Recommendations

### Weekly Content Production

**Monday:**
- LinkedIn post (early morning): Presentation tip or insight
- Engage with other consultants' LinkedIn content

**Tuesday:**
- Publish blog post or article on your site
- Share article on LinkedIn with additional commentary
- Newsletter preview snippet on LinkedIn

**Wednesday (or every other Wednesday):**
- Send newsletter (early morning)
- Share newsletter on LinkedIn
- Respond to comments and engagement

**Thursday:**
- LinkedIn post: Case study or example
- Engage with community

**Friday:**
- LinkedIn post: Weekly roundup or light content
- Prepare next week's content

**Weekend (for "Off the Clock" segment):**
- Schedule LinkedIn post for Sunday evening or Monday morning
- Light engagement

### Monthly Rhythm

- **Week 1:** Focus on foundational presentation skills
- **Week 2:** Focus on client presentation scenarios
- **Week 3:** Focus on tools and templates
- **Week 4:** Focus on advanced strategy or case studies

### Quarterly Review

- Analyze newsletter metrics (open rate, click-through, unsubscribe)
- Review LinkedIn analytics (engagement, reach)
- Adjust content strategy based on what resonates
- Prune email list to maintain quality over quantity

## Key Takeaway for SlideHeroes

**Consultants are not looking for more content—they're looking for content that helps them solve their presentation challenges and win more business.**

The winning strategy is:
1. **Educate first, sell second** - This is the approach that builds trust and authority
2. **Be consistent** - Build habits with your audience through predictable, valuable content
3. **Be authentic** - Share unique insights, not generic advice
4. **Be actionable** - Every piece of content should have practical takeaways
5. **Be where they are** - Email newsletter, LinkedIn, podcasts - the channels they already use and trust

By executing on this strategy, SlideHeroes can become the go-to resource for presentation strategy among individual consultants and small/medium consultancies.
