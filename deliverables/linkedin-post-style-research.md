## Platform Mechanics & Algorithm

### LinkedIn Algorithm Overview (2025-2026)

Based on analysis of 1.8M+ posts by Richard van der Blom and 621,833 posts by AuthoredUp:

#### Three-Stage Distribution
1. **Initial Classification (0-60 min)**: LinkedIn evaluates quality, relevance, spam indicators, decides test audience size
2. **Engagement Testing (1-2 hrs)**: Measures engagement velocity. Comments count 2x likes. Dwell time analyzed.
3. **Extended Distribution (2+ hrs)**: Expands reach based on early performance and relevance signals

#### Key Algorithm Signals (Ranked by Impact)

| Signal | Impact | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Saves | 5x reach vs. 1 like | Strongest quiet signal |
| Comments (indirect/discussion) | 2.4x reach boost | Replies to comments trigger this |
| Dwell time | Major signal | Time spent reading, even without interaction |
| First 60-90 min engagement | Critical window | Determines if post expands or dies |
| "See more" click | Positive signal | Indicates interest, but must deliver |
| Links (single) | Reach penalty | Sends users off platform |
| Links (4+) | 3-5x reach vs. single link | Signals value/curation |

#### What the Feed Contains (2025)
- Promoted Company Content: 28%
- Top Creator Content: 31%
- Other Creator Content: 28%
- LinkedIn Ads: 11%
- **Organic Company Content: 2%** (nearly invisible without sponsorship)

**Takeaway**: Personal profiles vastly outperform company pages. Build personal brand first.

### Device & Usage Stats

| Metric | Statistic | Implication |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Mobile access | 72% | Design for mobile first |
| Mobile engagement | 91% of browsing | Short, scannable posts win |
| Profile editing | 80% on desktop | Long-form in profile, short in posts |
| Vertical video growth | +80% YoY | 9:16 aspect ratio preferred |
| Vertical image CTR | +10% vs. square | Portrait orientation wins |

### Dwell Time Mechanics

LinkedIn tracks how long users spend reading—even without interaction. This is "dwell time."

**Best-performing dwell time posts have:**
- 300-400 words (not characters)
- 20+ sentences
- Formatted for scanning (bullets, breaks, bold)
- Story or lesson that gives reason to slow scroll

### The "Golden Hour" Strategy

The first 60-90 minutes after posting are critical:

| Time Window | Action | Why |
|-------------|--------|-----|
| 0-5 min | Be available to reply | Early comments signal quality |
| 5-30 min | Engage with every comment | Builds momentum |
| 30-60 min | Comment on 3-5 other posts | Signals activity to algorithm |
| 60-90 min | Continue engagement | Determines extended distribution |

**Key stat**: Posts with indirect comments (replies to comments, not just comments on post) get 2.4x reach.

### Content Lifespan in 2025

- Old rule: 24-hour platform
- New reality: Posts can get traction for 2-3 weeks
- Condition: Must spark meaningful conversations
- Posts with ongoing discussion appear in feeds longer

## Structure & Format

### Optimal Post Length

| Post Type | Character Count | Word Count | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|-------|
| Sweet spot | 800-1,000 chars | ~150-180 words | Best balance of depth and completion |
| Short posts | <500 chars | <90 words | Good for announcements, quick tips |
| Long-form (top 5%) | 1,500-2,500 chars | 300-400 words | Only if highly valuable |
| Text-only penalty | Any length | -41% usage, -18% engagement YoY | Avoid without visual |

**Reading level**: Aim for 4th grade. Posts above 10th grade get 35% less reach.

### Paragraph & Sentence Structure

**The LinkedIn Paragraph Rule:**
- Maximum 4 lines per paragraph
- 1-2 sentences per paragraph is ideal
- Single-line paragraphs are preferred for hooks and key points

**Sentence count sweet spot:**
- 16-20 sentences for optimal performance
- Short sentences (10-15 words) for rhythm
- Mix with occasional longer sentences for variety

**White space is content:**
- Break up text aggressively
- Empty lines between thoughts
- White space = readability = dwell time

### The "See More" Fold

**Character limit before truncation:**
- Desktop: ~300 characters (approximately 3 lines)
- Mobile: ~140 characters (approximately 2 lines)

**Strategy:**
- First 2 lines must hook without revealing everything
- Create tension that makes clicking "see more" irresistible
- The click itself is a positive algorithm signal

### Post Structure Template

```
LINE 1: Hook (stops the scroll)
LINE 2: Hook continuation (creates curiosity/tension)
[SEE MORE FALLS HERE]

Empty line

LINE 4-6: The story/insight setup (who, what, when)
Empty line
PARAGRAPH: The meat (the lesson, the framework, the value)
Empty line
BULLETS: Key takeaways (optional but powerful)
Empty line
LINE: Transition to CTA
Empty line
CTA: Question or invitation
Empty line
HASHTAGS: 3-5 max, end of post
```

### Lists vs. Prose

**When to use lists:**
- Frameworks and step-by-step processes
- Key takeaways
- Multiple tips
- Save-worthy content (lists get saved 3x more)

**When to use prose:**
- Storytelling
- Personal anecdotes
- Opinion pieces
- Emotional content

**Format rules for lists:**
- Maximum 5-7 items (readers lose interest after)
- Each item: 1-2 lines max
- Use emoji bullets for visual scanning
- Number if sequence matters

### CTA Placement & Format

**Best CTA formats (ranked by engagement):**

| CTA Type | Example | Engagement Lift |
|----------|---------|-----------------|
| Open question | "What's one strategy you've used to overcome this?" | Highest |
| Binary choice | "Agree or disagree?" | High |
| Experience ask | "Seen this happen in your industry?" | High |
| Tag prompt | "Tag someone who needs to hear this" | Medium |
| Link click | "Link in comments" | Low (but sometimes necessary) |

**Placement:**
- End of post (last 2-3 lines)
- Never bury in middle
- Make it the natural conclusion

## Writing Style

### LinkedIn Tone vs. Other Platforms

| Platform | Tone | LinkedIn Difference |
|----------|------|---------------------|
| Blog | Educational, thorough | LinkedIn is more conversational, less formal |
| Email | Direct, personal | LinkedIn is public—more polish needed |
| Twitter | Witty, brief | LinkedIn allows depth, professional context |
| Instagram | Visual, lifestyle | LinkedIn is value-first, achievement-oriented |

**The LinkedIn voice**: Professional but human. Expert but accessible. Opinionated but respectful.

### Sentence Rhythm

**The 1-3-1 pattern** (used by top LinkedIn writers):
1. Short sentence (punchy)
2. Medium sentence (explains)
3. Medium sentence (expands)
4. Short sentence (concludes section)

**Example:**
> Most content fails. (3 words)
> It fails because creators optimize for algorithms instead of humans. (10 words)
> They stuff keywords and use AI-generated hooks that feel robotic. (10 words)
> It shows. (2 words)

### Conversational Without Being Sloppy

**Do:**
- Write like you talk (use contractions: "you'll" not "you will")
- Address the reader directly ("you" not "one")
- Use active voice ("I built this" not "this was built")
- Share opinions ("I believe" not "it is thought")
- Include personality quirks and humor where appropriate

**Don't:**
- Use corporate speak ("leverage," "synergy," "circle back")
- Start with "In today's digital landscape..."
- Use AI tells ("In conclusion," "It's important to note," "Here are X things")
- Over-explain obvious concepts
- Be so casual you seem unprofessional

### Power Words & Phrases That Perform

**Opening power words:**
- "Stop" / "Don't" (commands attention)
- "I" (personal authority)
- "You" (direct address)
- Numbers ("3 ways," "47%")
- "The truth about..."
- "Here's what nobody tells you..."

**Emotional triggers:**
- "discovered" / "realized" (implies insight)
- "exactly" (implies specificity)
- "finally" (implies journey/struggle)
- "secret" / "hidden" (implies exclusivity)
- "uncomfortable truth" (implies honesty)

**Trust builders:**
- "In my experience..."
- "After testing this with 50+ clients..."
- "The data shows..."
- "I used to believe... until..."

### Words & Phrases to Avoid

**AI tells (instant credibility killers):**
- "In today's [adjective] [noun]..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Here are [X] ways to..."
- "Let's dive in"
- "Unlock the power of..."
- "Game-changer"

**Corporate jargon (makes eyes glaze over):**
- Leverage
- Synergy
- Circle back
- Bandwidth
- Low-hanging fruit
- Move the needle
- At scale
- Deep dive
- Drill down

**Engagement bait (can suppress reach):**
- "Like if you agree!"
- "Share this with someone who needs it"
- "Comment below!"
- "Tag 3 friends"
- Overly generic CTAs

### Being Opinionated Without Being Abrasive

**The "I" statement technique:**
- ❌ "Anyone who does X is wrong"
- ✅ "In my experience, X doesn't work. Here's why..."

**The "might" hedge:**
- ❌ "This strategy will fail"
- ✅ "This strategy might be holding you back"

**The "used to believe" frame:**
- ❌ "Stop believing X"
- ✅ "I used to believe X. Then I discovered..."

**The data anchor:**
- ❌ "X is better than Y"
- ✅ "In my tests, X outperformed Y by 40%. Your results may vary."

### Storytelling Techniques for LinkedIn

**The CAR Framework** (Context-Action-Result):
1. **Context**: Set the scene (who, when, what situation)
2. **Action**: What you did (the decision, the risk)
3. **Result**: What happened (the lesson, the outcome)

**The "Before/After" Arc:**
1. Before: The struggle/naive belief
2. Turning point: The discovery/realization
3. After: The transformation/new understanding

**The "Mistake Story" (highly engaging):**
1. "I made a $50K mistake"
2. Here's exactly what I did wrong
3. Here's what I learned
4. Here's how you can avoid it

**Story length rule:** Keep stories to 3-5 short paragraphs. Long stories lose readers.

## Formatting Mechanics

### Line Breaks & Spacing

**The rule:** Aggressive white space. If in doubt, add a line break.

**Best practices:**
- Empty line between every thought/paragraph
- Never more than 2 sentences without a break
- Empty line before CTA
- Empty line before hashtags

**Mobile rendering:**
- What looks spacious on desktop may look cramped on mobile
- Preview on mobile before posting
- Test with LinkedIn's mobile preview or AuthoredUp

### Emoji Usage

**Current consensus (2025):**
- Emojis increase engagement by 33%
- 83% of LinkedIn users believe emojis help express themselves
- Professional doesn't mean boring anymore

**Quantity rules:**
- 3-7 emojis per post is optimal
- Never more than 2 emojis per line
- Don't replace words with emojis ("📈💰" instead of "revenue increased")

**Best emoji categories for LinkedIn:**

| Category | Emojis | Use Case |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Growth/success | 📈 🚀 💰 🎯 | Results, achievements, goals |
| Ideas/innovation | 💡 ⚡ 🔥 💎 | Insights, tips, breakthroughs |
| Professional | 🤝 💼 🏆 ✨ | Networking, careers, excellence |
| Engagement | 👏 🙌 💪 👀 | Celebration, enthusiasm, attention |
| List bullets | ▸ ◦ → • | Organizing lists (cleaner than emoji bullets) |

**Where to place emojis:**
- Beginning of hook (1 emoji): "🚀 Just closed the biggest deal..."
- End of key statements: "...and that changed everything. 💡"
- As bullet points in lists
- In the CTA: "Drop a 🔥 if this resonated"
- NOT: Clumped together (😀👏🤩), in formal announcements, replacing text

**What to avoid:**
- Overly casual: 🤪 👯 🥴 💀
- Potentially misinterpreted: 👍 on serious topics
- Emoji-only comments (perform poorly)

### Hashtag Strategy

**The 2025 reality:** Hashtags have minimal impact on reach. LinkedIn disabled hashtag pages on desktop in Oct 2024. However, they still help with search discoverability.

**Quantity:**
- 3-5 hashtags optimal
- 1-3 has 12.6% engagement boost (CloselyHQ data)
- 6+ hashtags can suppress reach
- 0 hashtags is fine if content is strong

**Placement:**
- End of post (consensus best practice)
- Separate from main content with empty line
- Can embed naturally in text if relevant

**Hashtag mix strategy:**

| Type | Example | Purpose | Follower Count |
|------|---------|---------|----------------|
| Broad | #Marketing #Leadership | Wide reach | 10M+ |
| Niche | #ContentStrategy #B2BMarketing | Targeted audience | 100K-1M |
| Branded | #[YourBrand] | Community building | Varies |

**Format rules:**
- Use PascalCase: #ContentMarketing (not #contentmarketing)
- Accessibility: Screen readers pronounce correctly
- Avoid: Overly long hashtags, trending tags that don't fit

**Top 10 LinkedIn hashtags by followers (2025):**
1. #Innovation (38M+)
2. #Management (35M+)
3. #DigitalMarketing (27M+)
4. #Technology (26M+)
5. #Creativity (25M+)
6. #Entrepreneurship (22M+)
7. #Startups (21M+)
8. #Marketing (20M+)
9. #SocialMedia (19M+)
10. #Branding (18M+)

### Text Formatting (Bold, Italic)

**LinkedIn native support:** Limited. No native bold/italic in posts.

**Workarounds:**
- Unicode characters for bold (via tools like AuthoredUp, YayText)
- LinkedIn articles support full formatting
- LinkedIn newsletters support formatting

**Bold unicode examples:**
- **𝗕𝗼𝗹𝗱** (works but may not render on all devices)
- Use sparingly for key terms only
- Test on mobile before relying on it

**Best practice:** Use capitalization for emphasis instead:
- "THE TRUTH IS..."
- "Here's the KEY insight..."

### Links Strategy

**The link penalty:**
- Single external link = reach penalty
- LinkedIn wants to keep users on platform
- BUT: 4+ links perform 3-5x better than 1 link

**Link strategies ranked by reach impact:**

| Strategy | Penalty | Best For |
|----------|---------|----------|
| No link (pure value) | None | Thought leadership, tips |
| Link in first comment | Low | Blog posts, resources |
| Add link after comments start | Lowest | Time-sensitive content |
| Link in post, preview removed | Medium | When preview is essential |
| Link in post, preview on | High | Avoid when possible |
| 4+ curated links | Boost | Resource roundups, tool lists |

**The "link in comments" nuance:**
- Used to be the standard workaround
- 2025 data suggests it's less effective than before
- LinkedIn algorithm has adapted
- Still better than in-post link with preview

**Link format best practices:**
- Put value first, link last in caption
- "Here's the full guide: [link]" works better than "[link] - check this guide"
- Use UTM tags to track actual clicks
- Consider if link is truly necessary

### Images & Media

**Image post performance:**
- 1.18x reach multiplier (down from 1.22x)
- Images with people: +50% engagement
- Vertical images: +10% CTR vs. square

**Image specs:**
- Optimal: 1080x1350px (4:5 ratio) or 1080x1920px (9:16)
- Avoid: Horizontal images (perform worst on mobile)
- File size: <8MB
- Format: PNG or JPG

**When to use images:**
- Personal photos (you at events, speaking, working)
- Screenshots with annotations
- Simple graphics with text overlays
- Data visualizations

**When NOT to use images:**
- Random stock photos
- Overly designed graphics
- Images that don't add value
- Selfies that don't relate to content

### Tagging People

**When to tag:**
- When mentioning someone's work or quote
- When someone is directly involved in the story
- When responding to their comment
- In collab posts

**When NOT to tag:**
- Randomly to get attention
- "Tag someone who needs this" (engagement bait)
- Excessive tagging (looks spammy)

**Tag format:**
- First mention: Full name @profile
- Subsequent: First name only
- Never tag more than 5 people per post

## Hook Formulas

### Why Hooks Matter

- First 2-3 sentences determine if someone clicks "see more"
- Strong hooks boost reader retention by up to 30%
- AI-generated hooks are 16x more common (reducing uniqueness)
- Your hook must stand out as human and compelling

### Hook Formula Categories

---

#### 1. CURIOUSITY HOOKS

Create a knowledge gap that must be filled.

**Formula 1: The Teaser**
> "I discovered something that changed everything about how I [activity]."

**Formula 2: The Partial Reveal**
> "ONE small change in your [process] can [big result]. I tested it for [timeframe]."

**Formula 3: The Counterintuitive**
> "Everything you know about [topic] is wrong. Here's what actually works."

**Formula 4: The Mystery**
> "It was the decision that changed my career path forever."

**Examples:**
- "I discovered a LinkedIn strategy that felt like uncovering a secret society."
- "What happened next took me completely by surprise."
- "The difference between my two launches? One word."

---

#### 2. CONTRARIAN HOOKS

Challenge conventional wisdom. High risk, high reward.

**Formula 5: The Bold Rejection**
> "Most advice about [topic] is dead wrong. Here's why."

**Formula 6: The Unpopular Opinion**
> "Unpopular opinion: [contrary statement]. And here's why I stand by it."

**Formula 7: The Myth Buster**
> "Stop doing [common practice]. It's hurting your [goal]."

**Examples:**
- "Leadership books won't make you a better leader—real-world experience will."
- "The hustle culture is a lie. Here's what actually works."
- "Stop networking. Start doing this instead."

---

#### 3. STORY HOOKS

Draw readers into a narrative immediately.

**Formula 8: The Failure**
> "I lost [significant thing] because of [mistake]. Here's what I learned."

**Formula 9: The Transformation**
> "I was exactly where you are now—[current state]. Here's how I got to [desired state]."

**Formula 10: The Moment**
> "The first time I [significant action], I learned an invaluable lesson."

**Formula 11: The Surprising Result**
> "My biggest failure turned into my most treasured success."

**Examples:**
- "Losing my job was the best thing that ever happened to me."
- "The first time I spoke at a conference, I learned an invaluable lesson."
- "I was exactly where you are now, skeptical but hopeful."

---

#### 4. DATA HOOKS

Lead with compelling numbers to establish authority.

**Formula 12: The Shocking Stat**
> "[X]% of [group] [unexpected outcome]. Here's why."

**Formula 13: The Personal Result**
> "I [action] and got [specific result] in [timeframe]. Here's exactly how."

**Formula 14: The Industry Insight**
> "According to [source], [statistic]. But here's what nobody's talking about..."

**Examples:**
- "Only 10% of startups make it past the first year. Here's why."
- "75% of professionals believe personal branding influences career success."
- "A staggering 90% of consumers say authenticity is key when choosing brands."

---

#### 5. QUESTION HOOKS

Engage the reader's curiosity directly.

**Formula 15: The Direct Question**
> "Ever wondered why [common problem]?"

**Formula 16: The Challenge**
> "Do you know the #1 mistake most [group] make on [platform]?"

**Formula 17: The "What If"**
> "What if I told you [counterintuitive statement]?"

**Examples:**
- "Ever feel like your content disappears into the void? Here's why."
- "Do you know the number one mistake most marketers make on LinkedIn?"
- "What if I told you networking could be effortless?"

---

#### 6. DIRECT ADDRESS HOOKS

Speak straight to the reader's situation.

**Formula 18: The Empathy**
> "You're not alone in feeling [emotion] about [situation]."

**Formula 19: The Call Out**
> "You might be the reason your team [positive/negative outcome]. Here's why."

**Formula 20: The Permission**
> "You have permission to [contrary action]. Here's why it's actually the right move."

**Examples:**
- "You're not alone in feeling overwhelmed by digital marketing."
- "You have the power to transform your LinkedIn presence today."
- "You're probably making this mistake without realizing it."

---

### Hook Selection by Content Pillar

| Pillar Type | Best Hook Categories | Why |
|-------------|---------------------|-----|
| Educational | Data, Question, Curiosity | Promises specific value |
| Story/Experience | Story, Contrarian | Personal authority |
| Opinion/Take | Contrarian, Direct Address | Sparks debate |
| How-To/Guide | Curiosity, Data | Demonstrates expertise |
| Achievement/Win | Data, Story | Proof of results |
| Framework/List | Curiosity, Direct Address | Implies actionable value |
| Vulnerability/Fail | Story (Failure), Direct Address | Humanizes, builds trust |

### Hook Writing Checklist

- [ ] First line is under 15 words
- [ ] Creates curiosity, tension, or immediate value
- [ ] Avoids AI tells ("In today's...", "Here are X ways...")
- [ ] Uses active voice
- [ ] Contains a power word (discover, secret, truth, exactly)
- [ ] Doesn't give away the full story
- [ ] Works on mobile (visible before "see more")

## Content Types & When to Use

### Performance Rankings (Personal Profiles, 2025)

| Rank | Content Type | Reach Multiplier | Trend | Best For |
|------|--------------|------------------|-------|----------|
| 1 | Polls | 1.64x | +24% YoY | Quick engagement, market research |
| 2 | Documents (Carousels) | 1.45x | Stable | Frameworks, guides, processes |
| 3 | Images | 1.18x | -27% reach | Personal photos, announcements |
| 4 | Video | 1.10x | -28% reach | Behind-the-scenes, tutorials |
| 5 | Text-only | 0.88x | -28% reach | Rare occasions only |

### Polls

**When to use:**
- Market research
- Gathering opinions
- Sparking debate (binary choices work well)
- Quick engagement boost

**Best practices:**
- 3 answer options (performs best)
- 7-day duration (outperforms 1-3 days)
- Follow up with your own analysis/reflection
- Don't overuse (looks spammy)

### Documents/Carousels (PDFs)

**When to use:**
- Frameworks and methodologies
- Step-by-step tutorials
- Data visualizations
- Evergreen content (high save rate)

**Best practices:**
- 8-10 slides optimal (15 max)
- First slide = hook (must grab attention)
- Simple design, text-focused
- Include screenshots and examples
- Strong visual storytelling throughout

**Design rules:**
- Half solid color background (don't over-design)
- Text is the focus, not graphics
- Each slide: one idea, minimal text
- Final slide: CTA or summary

### Videos

**When to use:**
- Behind-the-scenes
- Speaking clips
- Quick tips/tutorials
- Product demos
- Personal stories with visual element

**Best practices:**
- Under 60 seconds (30s ideal)
- Vertical format (9:16)
- Good thumbnail is critical
- Short, snappy editing
- Captions (85% watch without sound)

### Images

**When to use:**
- Personal photos (you at events, speaking)
- Screenshots with annotations
- Simple graphics
- Data visualizations
- Team/company announcements

**Best practices:**
- Include yourself in photos (+50% engagement)
- Vertical orientation (4:5 or 9:16)
- Clear, simple visuals
- Relevant to the text content
- Avoid generic stock photos

### Text-Only Posts

**When to use:**
- Highly emotional content
- Strong storytelling
- Controversial opinions
- Quick announcements
- Only when visual doesn't add value

**Best practices:**
- Must be exceptionally well-written
- Strong hook even more critical
- Aggressive formatting (white space)
- Rarely: maybe 1 in 10 posts

## Expert Wisdom Compilation

### From Jasmin Alic (LinkedIn Ghostwriter)

**Key principles:**
1. **Hook first, everything else second**: The first line determines 80% of post success
2. **Write like you talk, edit like a pro**: Conversational but polished
3. **One idea per post**: Trying to cover too much dilutes impact
4. **The "truth" test**: Would you say this to a friend over coffee?
5. **Specificity wins**: "I made $47,283" beats "I made five figures"

**Formatting insights:**
- Use the "breathing room" rule: Add line breaks you think are too many, then add one more
- Start with the conclusion, then explain (inverted pyramid)
- Use bold (unicode) for key terms only
- Each paragraph should be a single idea

**Hook advice:**
- The hook isn't about being clever—it's about being relevant
- Address the reader's current pain or desired gain
- "You" is the most powerful word in hooks
- Test hooks: Would YOU click "see more"?

### From Lara Acosta (0 to 57K in 1 Year)

**Key principles:**
1. **Personal branding ≠ oversharing**: It's not a diary
2. **Consistency beats perfection**: Show up daily, learn, iterate
3. **Soft selling works**: Add value for months before asking for anything
4. **Unique voice is your differentiator**: People follow YOU, not your knowledge

**Format advice:**
- Write for single-line reading (break up paragraphs)
- Content should sound like a conversation
- Carousels are the best growth tool right now
- Keep carousel covers simple—text is the star

**Common mistakes to avoid:**
- Oversharing personal problems unrelated to business
- Chasing viral trends that don't fit your brand
- Being boring/super-professional out of fear
- Posting selfies without relevant context

### From Richard van der Blom (Algorithm Researcher)

**Key findings from 1.8M+ post analysis:**
1. **Reach is down 34% in 2025**: Feeds are crowded, quality bar is higher
2. **Posts have a long tail**: 2-3 weeks visibility if they spark conversation
3. **Save-worthy content performs best**: Lists, guides, frameworks
4. **Comments are currency**: Especially replies to comments (not just comments)
5. **Company pages are nearly invisible**: 2% of feed, focus on personal profiles

**Timing insights:**
- First 60-90 minutes critical
- No universal best time—analyze your audience
- Don't post twice in 24 hours
- 3-5 posts per week is optimal

### From Our Expert Evaluations (Mckenna Boyd, Antonmaker, Giolaga)

**From Mckenna Boyd:**
- Authenticity > polish
- Share failures, not just wins
- Be helpful, not promotional
- Consistency compounds

**From Antonmaker:**
