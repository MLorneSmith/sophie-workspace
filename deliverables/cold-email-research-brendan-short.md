## 1. Summary of Approach & Philosophy

### Core Philosophy: Signal-Based Selling

Brendan J Short coined the term "signal-based selling" in late 2023. His fundamental thesis:

> **"Timing is 10x more important than copy. Maybe 100x."**

The old playbook (2011-2023) is dead:
- Spray-and-pray mass email to thousands of contacts
- "Triple tap" cold calls
- Generic personalization tokens ({first_name}, {company})
- Hiring armies of SDRs to hit volume quotas

**The new way:**
- Use technology to scan across *hundreds* of signals to determine WHO to reach out to THIS WEEK
- Let signals determine both the target AND the message
- Quality over quantity — "100 contacts to get 10 meetings" vs old "1,000 contacts to get 1 meeting"

### Key Principle: The Signal-Based Market (SBM)

> "Your SBM is the segment of your market that is most likely to take a sales meeting with you, today."

- TAM changes once a year
- SBM changes **daily**

The critical question every rep should ask:
> "What is the specific reason why I'm reaching out to this particular company/person, **today**?"

### The Great Signal Divide (2025-2026)

Brendan identifies two tiers of signals:

| Commodity Signals | Alpha/Niche Signals |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Job changes | Tech stack from job descriptions |
| Funding rounds | Internal project initiatives |
| Website visits | Custom AI-detected patterns |
| Generic intent data | First-party product usage patterns |
| Everyone has access | Unique to your company/market |

> "Generic signals—job changes, funding rounds, website visits—are becoming commoditized. Everyone has access to the same data from the same providers. And prospects can smell the automation from a mile away."

### LinkedIn > Cold Email (Current Recommendation)

Brendan and his collaborators (VibeScaling) strongly recommend LinkedIn-first outreach in 2025-2026:
- Max out connections weekly with ICPs
- DM 50 people/day asking for feedback
- Build founder brand through content
- "In the AI slop era, attention is scarce. Own your audience."

## 2. Specific Email Examples (With Analysis)

### The Core Cold Email Framework

Brendan uses a 4-part framework taught to him as an SDR:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. CONNECTION POINT (Why you? Why now?)        │
│  2. PAIN (What problem do they have?)           │
│  3. GAIN (What's the value/insight you offer?)  │
│  4. CTA (What's the next step?)                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Alternative framework: **AIDA** (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

---

### Email Teardown #1: Cursor (AI Coding Tool)
**Rating: 7/10**

**Subject:** Saw your AI tooling req

```
Hey {{first_name}},

Noticed {{company}} is hiring devs with AI coding tool experience. 
Sounds like you're serious about building this into how the team works, 
not just letting people experiment.

That's the hard part. Most eng orgs I talk to are stuck between 
"everyone uses whatever" and "we should probably have a strategy here."

Happy to share what's working for teams making that shift if useful.
```

**Why it works:**
- ✅ Specific signal: Hiring devs with AI coding experience
- ✅ Human tone: "devs" not "developers", "req" not "requisition"
- ✅ Short sentences, contractions
- ✅ Gives value (sharing insights), doesn't just ask for time

**AI or Human?** Split — some thought AI, some thought human. That's the goal.

---

### Email Teardown #2: Lovable (Low-Code Platform)
**Rating: "Slop" (Called out as bad AI copy)**

**Subject:** React + Supabase + Tailwind

```
Hey {{first_name}},

Was reading {{company}}'s job post mentioning React, Supabase, and Tailwind. 
That's exactly the stack Lovable outputs out of the box.

Curious if your team's explored using AI to scaffold new features or spin 
up internal tools faster. A few companies with similar stacks have cut 
their prototyping time from weeks to hours.

Worth a quick chat to see if it fits your workflow?
```

**Why it fails:**
- ❌ "Was reading" — robotic AI tell
- ❌ "That's exactly" — obvious AI phrase
- ❌ "Curious if your team's explored" — no human talks like this
- ❌ "Worth a quick chat?" — asking for time, not giving value

---

### Email Teardown #3: Attention (CRM Automation)
**Subject:** Saw you run MEDDIC

```
Hey {{first_name}},

Since {{company}} uses MEDDIC, I bet your reps struggle to update those 
fields after every call (but there's gold when they do).

Attention auto-populates MEDDIC (and whatever other frameworks or fields 
you use) straight from the conversation. No manual entry, no CRM hygiene 
nagging, no end-of-quarter data cleanup scrambles.

Worth 15 mins to see if it fits how your team runs?
```

**What works:**
- ✅ Clear signal: MEDDIC usage
- ✅ Relatable pain: Reps hate updating CRM fields
- ✅ Specific value prop: Auto-populates from conversation

**Brendan's critique:** "I don't love 'Worth 15 mins?' as a CTA. Asking for time in a cold email is a big ask."

### Email Teardown #4: Stripe (Cross-Border Payments)
**Subject:** Saw your post on cross-border payments

```
Hey {{first_name}},

Just read your LinkedIn post about the headaches of paying international 
contractors, so I figured this might be timely.

Stripe just rolled out Stablecoin Financial Accounts that lets you hold, 
receive, and send stablecoins in 101 countries. Settlements are near-instant, 
and transaction costs are about half what you're paying on traditional 
cross-border rails.

If international payments are a growing line item, might be worth a look.

Happy to walk through how it works if useful.
```

**Mixed reception:**
- ✅ Solid signal (LinkedIn post about payments)
- ✅ Good timing
- ❌ Copy feels dense
- ❌ "Might be worth a look" is weak

### AI Copy Tells to Avoid (2026)

The bar rises every few months. Current AI tells:

| Tell | Why It's Bad |
|------|--------------|
| "Was reading" | No one says this naturally |
| "That's exactly" | Obviously AI-assembled |
| "Curious if your team's explored" | Robotic phrasing |
| "Noticed" | Becoming a tell (was fine in 2024) |
| M-dashes (—) | AI overuses these |
| Overly structured sentences | Feels assembled, not written |

> "By default I assume every outreach email is AI from now on." — Audience member at AI x GTM Summit

### The "Proactive Value" Principle

**Stop asking, start giving.**

Bad CTAs (asking):
- "Worth 15 minutes?"
- "Open to a quick chat?"
- "Would you be interested in a demo?"

Good CTAs (giving):
- Share a CRM audit showing their data gaps
- Send 2-3 candidate profiles that match their job req
- Provide an insight about their market/competitors

**Example from Bravado:**
When a VP of Sales opens an Enterprise AE job req, Bravado doesn't pitch their database. They query it and send:
> "Hey, you should chat with Sarah, Mary, and John. Here are their profiles. Want to see more like them?"

The VP gets actual candidates. Their question ("Are they any good?") is answered before they ask.

## 3. Frameworks and Principles

### A. Signal Categories

**Company-Level Signals:**
- Raised funding
- Purchased relevant technology
- Hiring for specific roles
- New product launches
- Competitor mentions in job descriptions

**Person-Level Signals:**
- Job change
- Engaged with content (social/marketing)
- Product usage patterns
- Previous customer/champion at new company
- Website visits

**1st Party Signals (Your IP, not commoditized):**
- Product usage data
- Marketing email engagement
- Website visitors (who didn't fill forms)
- Closed-lost deals (resurrect opportunities)
- Gong call recordings
- CRM data

### B. Micro-Campaigns Framework

Definition:
- Small, highly-targeted lists: 50-250 contacts
- Generated using *very* specific filtering/signals
- Relevant THIS WEEK, stale next month
- Quality over quantity

**Micro-Campaign Example (Recruiting Services):**

Build a list using "rich" data points:
1. **Funding** (waterfalled from multiple sources)
2. **SaaS company?** (AI agent trained to identify)
3. **Year founded** (older = worse fit)
4. **Specific title present** at company
5. **Marketing email engagement** (last 30 days)
6. **ICP + Buyer Persona enrichment** (filter non-fits)
7. **Open job rec** (posted in last 5 days)

Run this engine 24/7. Low volume, high targeting, 10x conversion vs spray-and-pray.

### C. The GTM Engineer Role

A new role emerging at the intersection of RevOps, SDR, and Growth:

| Skill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Data proficiency | Analytics platforms, large datasets |
| Tech savvy | CRMs, automation tools, AI platforms |
| Process optimization | Streamline, identify inefficiencies |
| Strategic + Creative | 30,000ft view AND nitty-gritty details |
| Cross-department comms | Bridge sales, marketing, ops, data teams |

Experience required: 6-8+ years (not for junior SDRs)

### D. Human-in-the-Loop Principle

For cold outbound to accounts you care about:
- AI does 90% (research, drafting, prioritization)
- Human does last 10% (review, tweak, send)

**Exception:** Lower-stakes, higher-volume (inbound, event follow-ups, Tier 3 accounts) can be more automated.

### E. Deliverability Must-Dos

From Brendan's email checklist:
- Keep subject lines short (3 words or less ideal)
- Make subject lines conversational, not "markety"
- Use warming/rotation tools (Instantly, Smartlead)
- Text-only signatures on secondary domains (no images/links)
- Disable Open and Click tracking (hurts deliverability)
- Spin up multiple domains/mailboxes (not sustainable long-term but necessary now)

## 7. Key Takeaways for Implementation

### If You're Starting Cold Email Today:

1. **Don't start with cold email.** Start with LinkedIn.
   - Build content/brand first
   - Max connections with ICP
   - DM for feedback, not pitches

2. **If you must cold email:**
   - Focus 80% of effort on signal detection (WHO/WHEN)
   - Only 20% on copy (WHAT)
   - Use specific, niche signals — not generic ones
   - Give value, don't ask for time

3. **Build micro-campaigns, not blast lists:**
   - 50-250 contacts per campaign
   - Time-sensitive signals
   - Creative, company-specific targeting

4. **Human in the loop:**
   - AI drafts, human reviews
   - Especially for high-value accounts
   - The final 10% matters

5. **Track these metrics:**
   - Signal → meeting conversion rate
   - Micro-campaign performance
   - Deliverability (open rates, reply rates)
