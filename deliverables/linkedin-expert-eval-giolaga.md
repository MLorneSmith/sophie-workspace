## 2. Core Philosophy & Approach

### The Central Thesis
**"Code is a depreciating asset. Reputation is compound interest."**

Gio's core belief: Most engineers over-invest in technical skills and under-invest in visibility. His framework:

1. **The 5% Rule:** Spend 95% of time being a great engineer, 5% documenting and sharing
2. **Surface Area of Luck:** Luck = [Doing] × [Telling]. Engineers who never "tell" have zero luck surface area
3. **Proof of Work:** Create artifacts that work for you while you sleep — posts, articles, projects
4. **Warm vs Cold Market:** Build your network BEFORE you need it. Don't wait until you're laid off

### Key Mental Models He Uses
- **"Code vs Reputation"** — Technical skills depreciate; reputation compounds
- **"Media Company of One"** — You're not just a coder, you're a media company selling expertise
- **"O(1) Scannability"** — Content should be scannable, not dense (engineering metaphor)
- **"Vitamin vs Painkiller"** — Build solutions people urgently need, not nice-to-haves

### His Audience Philosophy
He specifically targets engineers who:
- Hate the idea of "selling themselves"
- Think LinkedIn is fake/cringey
- Don't know what to post about
- Feel their work speaks for itself (it doesn't)

## 3. Key Frameworks & Tactics

### A. LinkedIn Algorithm Mastery (The 5 Rules)

From his post "Why Nobody Sees Your Posts":

| Signal | What to Do | Why |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| **Dwell Time** | Structure posts for longer reading — hooks, bullets, carousels | Algorithm measures time spent viewing |
| **Comment Weight** | End with specific questions, reply to everyone | 1 comment = 4-7 likes in algorithm value |
| **First 60 Min Velocity** | Post and stick around for 15 min, reply immediately | Algorithm runs a "test" in first hour |
| **External Link Penalty** | Put links in comments, not post body. Use "zero-click" approach | LinkedIn wants to keep users on platform |
| **Consistency** | Post weekly forever, not daily for a month then quit | Algorithm builds a creator reliability profile |

### B. The "New Connection Honeymoon" Strategy
- When you connect with someone, LinkedIn prioritizes your content in their feed for 24-48 hours
- **Tactic:** Accept connection requests in batches, then post shortly after
- **Reverse tactic:** Add more people when you know a good post is going live

### C. Profile View Echo
- Visiting someone's profile registers as interest in the algorithm
- **Tactic:** Before reaching out to a recruiter/prospect, view their profile and engage with their recent posts first

### D. Post Formatting Framework ("O(1) Readability")

| Element | Rule | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Hook** | Value in line 1 (first 3 lines ~200 chars) | "We deleted all recurring meetings on Wednesdays. Productivity doubled." |
| **Whitespace** | Use Enter aggressively. One idea = one block | Don't write dense paragraphs |
| **Visual Anchors** | Use emojis as icons: 👉 ✅ 🚫 🧵 | Guide the eye through logic |
| **Lists** | Replace narrative with bullet comparisons | "Listening > Speaking, Delegating > Doing" |
| **Active Voice** | Subject → Verb → Object | Remove "It was observed that..." fluff |
| **You vs I** | Make reader the protagonist | "If you want to reduce AWS bill..." vs "I reduced..." |

### E. 10 Content Ideas for Engineers

1. **Problem → Solution** — "I was stuck on [Problem], discovered [Solution]"
2. **Project Launches** — Feature shipped, your specific contribution, technical challenge
3. **Industry Opinions** — Hot takes on trends, respectfully critical
4. **Tool Recommendations** — VS Code extensions, terminal shortcuts
5. **Team Dynamics** — Code review process, meeting efficiency, colleague shout-outs
6. **Event Coverage** — Conferences, meetups, team-building with 3 bullet takeaways
7. **Failure Posts** — 20% mistake, 80% fix and lesson learned
8. **Advice to Past Self** — Career tips, interview advice, onboarding
9. **Company Culture** — How your team works, pair programming, hack days
10. **Side Projects** — "Building in public" with ugly work-in-progress shots

### F. The "Coder to Builder" Transition

His framework for career resilience in the AI era:

1. **Own Your Unfair Advantage** — You can debug AI output; non-engineers can't
2. **Elevate from Executor to Architect** — Focus on Product, Architecture, User Value
3. **Start Side Projects** — AI has lowered building cost to near zero
4. **Document Publicly** — "I was there and I fixed it" stories are scarce and valuable
5. **Build Inbound** — Let opportunities come to you instead of begging outbound

## 4. Specific Examples & Templates

### Hook Templates

**❌ Bad Hook (High Latency):**
> "Hello network, I hope you are having a productive week. Today I would like to share some thoughts regarding our new meeting policy..."

**✅ Good Hook (Low Latency):**
> "We deleted all recurring meetings on Wednesdays. Productivity didn't just go up. It doubled."

### Problem-Solution Post Template
```
I was stuck on [Problem] for hours.

I discovered [Solution].

Here's why it works better:
- [Reason 1]
- [Reason 2]

💡 Pro Tip: Don't just share the solution. Share the pain you felt before finding it.
```

### Project Launch Template
```
We just shipped [Feature X].

My role was building the [Specific Part].

The biggest technical challenge was [Challenge].

We solved it by [Solution].

[GIF or screenshot of feature]
```

### Industry Opinion Template
```
I read about [Trend/Change in Tool X].

I really like [Feature A] because it solves [Problem].

But I'm not sure about [Feature B].

Has anyone tried it in production yet?
```

### Failure Post Template
```
I broke production yesterday.

Here's what happened: [Brief context]

How we fixed it: [Solution]

Process we put in place so it doesn't happen again: [Lesson]

Rule: 20% on the mistake, 80% on the fix.
```

### The "Alice vs Bob" Story Format
Gio uses parables effectively. His most viral concept:

> Two engineers, identical on paper (10 years experience, same skills).
> 
> **Bob** puts head down, crushes tickets, no LinkedIn presence. Gets laid off → applies to 50 jobs → competes with 5,000 identical resumes → waits months.
>
> **Alice** spends 5% of time documenting work, builds modest audience. Gets laid off → posts one update → DMs full of engineering managers who've been reading her posts → 3 offers by Friday.
>
> **Lesson:** You're not just a coder. You're a media company of one.
