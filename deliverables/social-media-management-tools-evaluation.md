## Reddit: Why Manual Is Better

**Reddit's API restrictions and community norms make automation problematic:**

1. **Authenticity is currency** — Redditors detect and punish corporate automation
2. **Subreddit rules vary** — Automated posting often violates community guidelines
3. **Karma requirements** — New accounts need manual participation to earn posting rights
4. **API limits** — Reddit restricts automated posting to prevent spam

**Recommended Reddit Strategy:**
1. **Manual engagement** — Reply to relevant threads authentically
2. **No scheduling** — Post when you have something valuable to add
3. **Build karma first** — Participate before promoting
4. **Human review required** — Never auto-post to Reddit

**Tools that claim Reddit support:**
- PostEverywhere.ai (limited, may violate TOS)
- Sked Social (limited)
- Manual + reminder systems (recommended)

## Tool Deep Dives

### Buffer

**Pros:**
- Dead simple UX
- Reliable posting (doesn't fail)
- Free tier is genuinely useful
- Good LinkedIn support
- Browser extension for quick saves

**Cons:**
- Limited AI features
- No Reddit support
- Analytics are basic
- No content recycling

**Verdict:** Best starting point. Upgrade only when you hit limits.

### PostEverywhere.ai

**Pros:**
- AI content adaptation per platform
- Multi-platform including Reddit (limited)
- Lead tracking
- Bulk scheduling

**Cons:**
- Newer tool (less proven)
- $29/mo minimum
- Reddit support may conflict with Reddit TOS

**Verdict:** Consider when scaling multi-platform presence.

### Hootsuite

**Pros:**
- Industry standard
- Social listening
- Enterprise security
- Extensive integrations

**Cons:**
- $99/mo minimum
- UI feels dated
- Overkill for small teams
- Learning curve

**Verdict:** Only for enterprise or agencies. Overkill for SlideHeroes now.

### Sprout Social

**Pros:**
- Best analytics in class
- Sentiment analysis
- Beautiful reports
- Strong competitor tracking

**Cons:**
- $249/mo per user (!)
- Way overpowered for small teams
- ROI hard to justify early-stage

**Verdict:** Maybe someday. Not now.

## Conclusion

**Start simple:**
1. Buffer Free for LinkedIn
2. Manual for Reddit
3. Claude/GPT for AI content
4. Upgrade only when proven ROI

**Budget-conscious path:** $0-6/month covers everything for SlideHeroes' current scale.

**Don't overpay for features you won't use.** Most "enterprise" social tools are designed for agencies managing dozens of client accounts. For a focused B2B SaaS like SlideHeroes, a lean stack is more effective and far cheaper.
