## Expert 1: Sam McKenna (#samsales)

### Background

### Core Philosophy: "Show Me You Know Me" (SMYKM)

Sam's trademarked methodology is built on the principle that **authentic personalization earns 13x the reply rates compared to industry average**.

**The Core Belief:**
> Only 8% of businesses use personalization effectively (McKinsey research). This is your competitive advantage.

**The Philosophy in Practice:**
- Manners-first, buyer-first approach
- Stand out from the "sea of sameness" in buyers' inboxes
- Authentically connect throughout your outreach
- Never sacrifice authenticity for automation

---

### Tactical Frameworks

#### 1. The Cold Email Framework

**Step 1: Nail the Subject Line**
> "If you tank this, crafting the perfect email body is pointless!"

- Use SMYKM in the subject line
- Reference specific, personal details about the prospect
- Example: Drum emoji + Rush lyric for a prospect who loves Rush

**Step 2: Create a Hook**
- Be an authentic human
- Reference a real connection with the prospect
- Ask: "How can you stand out from the bajillion other sales emails?"

**Step 3: Anticipate the Objection**
- Don't just acknowledge objections—respond to them before the prospect can dismiss you
- Embed the objection-response within your email

**Step 4: The Critical Rule**
> "PLEASE! Do not send a calendar link in the initial email. We beg you."

#### 2. LinkedIn Personalization Research Process

**The SMYKM Research Method:**
1. Research 3-5 specific details per recipient:
   - Recent LinkedIn posts
   - Company news
   - Mutual connections
   - Shared experiences
   - Personal interests (from profile)

2. Create template frameworks with placeholder spots for personalization
3. Never send a generic message—every message must prove you did your homework

#### 3. C-Suite/Executive Selling Approach

**Key Statistics Sam Cites:**
- 4X engagement increase when content posted by C-suite
- 92% brand trustworthiness increase when executives are active on LinkedIn
- 6X audience conversion rate when exposed to brand messages on LinkedIn
- 73% of decision-makers trust thought leadership over company marketing materials

**The Executive Approach:**
- Target your ideal buyers with proven social selling methodology
- Customer connections + Profile view monitoring + Strategic earned media commenting
- Focus on being a thought leader, not a salesperson

#### 4. LinkedIn Executive Branding Framework

**Content Pillars for Executives:**
1. Capture the executive's unique tone, POV, insights
2. Monitor current events and industry trends proactively
3. Develop content optimized for LinkedIn's algorithm
4. Manage DMs, connection requests, profile views systematically

**Why Personal Profiles Beat Company Pages:**
> "People want to buy from people, not brands. Company pages average 6 likes for every 100,000 followers. Your personal page gives a face to your organization."

Alex's approach is fundamentally about **building trust through visible expertise**.

**The Core Belief:**

**Key Insight:**

### The 3 Principles of Social Selling

#### Principle 1: Demonstrate Credibility

**What This Means:**
- Use LinkedIn as a journal of what you've learned
- Document your process publicly (wins, losses, learnings)
- Don't discount the value of what you know

**How to Execute:**
1. Share knowledge from your company and customers
2. Verbally extract knowledge from subject matter experts in your company
3. "Grab those insights and run with them for dear life"

**Example - Sarah Brazier (Gong AE):**
Document your sales process publicly. Be a "seasoned pro at publicly documenting your process."

#### Principle 2: Highlight Customer Results

**The Two Secrets:**

**Secret 1: Provide Specific, Crispy Numbers**
- "Increased SQLs by 25%"
- "Slashed costs by 70%"
- "Skyrocketed ARR by 65%"

**Secret 2: Leave Something Out**
> "Give them a blurry part of the photograph, a cliffhanger, a gap. Make readers have a burning question they need answered from you. It creates a beautiful emotional reaction. Make them ask you for more details."

**The Case Study Framework:**
1. Talk about the attitude that drove results
2. Share the outcome (with numbers)
3. Tell the story
4. **Leave out exactly HOW you did it**

> "People will literally say 'I'll bite—how'd you do it?'"

#### Principle 3: Slow Down Interactions

**What This Means:**
- Social selling is NOT an email blast
- Don't rush into DMs and pitch
- Breaking psychological safety kills deals

**How to Execute:**
1. Leave good impressions with simple responses to comments
2. If someone likes 3 of your posts, research them, then engage relevantly
3. "Listen to your gut and when you get to that balance, you just sort of feel it"
4. "I just sort of waft the food in front of them. I don't try to spear them."

**Patience Pays:**
Alex shared an example of a prospect responding to a message sent **3 years earlier**.

### Tactical Frameworks

#### 1. The Content Strategy

**What Works (Alex's Data):**
- Educational posts
- Inspiring/motivational content
- Contributory content (helps readers learn)
- Posts that don't mention your company

**What Doesn't Correlate with Lead Gen:**
- Topic of the post (surprisingly!)
- Posts that are purely promotional

**The Balance:**
> "If you only try to convert all the time, it'll fatigue people. If you only let people get to know you, then you have a fanbase with no revenue."

#### 2. The Origin Story First Post

**Why It Works:**
- Your network hasn't heard your full story
- Easy for audience to engage with
- Everyone can cheer you on
- Provides context for everything else you build

**The Interview Process for Content:**
1. Ask the origin question: "Why did you start this company?"
2. Ask clarifying questions
3. Dig into follow-up questions (where the gold is)
4. "Create poetry, not prose" - pack value into every line

#### 3. The Engagement Strategy

**Before You Post:**
- Comment on other people's posts (especially right before your post)
- Connect with new people
- Exchange DMs (these people see your content first)

**The "Karma System":**
> "You have not obeyed the karma system that is LinkedIn algorithm. You're essentially deemed selfish—post and ghost."

**After You Post:**
- Respond to all comments (except inane ones)
- Don't send connection requests thanking people for engaging
- If there's something genuine about their profile, mention it
- "Treat it like a conference or coffee shop"

#### 4. The Network-Building Strategy

**Counterintuitive Insight:**
> "You actually get more inbound lead gen by engaging with other people in your space—other consultants, software companies that work with your market—than by trying to engage with just your prospects' posts."

**The Stage Analogy:**
> "Think of your target market as people sitting in the audience. You want to be onstage speaking or on the panel, asking questions of the panel. You don't want to be going through the audience."

#### 5. The Follow-Up Framework

**For Post Engagers:**
1. Did they comment? Respond publicly multiple times before going to DMs
2. Did they just like? Skip unless there's something genuinely relevant
3. If DMing: Reference what they engaged with + something from their background

**The Key Test:**
> "Don't feel obligated. If you're thinking 'this doesn't feel right,' listen to that and skip it. You're better off following up when there's a good reason."

**The Expertise-Revealing Question:**
> "Ask questions that, by the asking, reveal your expertise. 'How's your SEO going?' doesn't reveal expertise. 'How have you been managing backlink distribution?' does."

### Key Principles & Rules

1. **Quality > Frequency**
   > "I'd rather have you post once a week with really good stuff than post four times a week with a mix of quality."

2. **Don't Try to Go Viral**
   > "Virality often boils the ocean. The best pipeline is usually not created from virality—it's from a few inquiries, not hundreds."

3. **Share Your Secret Sauce**
   > "No one's gonna steal your secret sauce. If they try, it'll be watered down. I've been posting our 'secret sauce' for 6 years. No one's stolen it because we execute better."

4. **Ask Expertise-Revealing Questions**
   Your questions should demonstrate knowledge by their very framing.

5. **Engagement is Undervalued**
   > "The biggest thing people miss is they let it sit. People comment, and they let it sit."

6. **Your Followers Count Doesn't Matter**
   Alex generated millions with 2,000-12,000 followers. Content quality > audience size.

7. **Don't Pitch in DMs**
   Wait. Build the relationship. The right moment will come.

## Key Takeaways for SlideHeroes

1. **Personalization Wins**: Sam's SMYKM approach earns 13x better reply rates. Every outreach to consultancy leaders must prove you did your homework.

2. **Credibility Compounds**: Alex's $5M+ came from consistent value-posting over years. Your ex-Mastercard background is a credibility marker—use it.

3. **Slow Down**: Both experts emphasize patience. Don't pitch immediately. Build trust through helpful content first.

4. **Engagement > Posting**: Alex says top creators spend more time engaging than posting. Comment on consultancy thought leaders' posts consistently.

5. **The Curiosity Gap**: Leave something out of case studies. Make them ask "how?"

6. **Quality Over Frequency**: One great post per week beats 5 mediocre ones.

7. **Personal Profile > Company Page**: C-suite posts get 4X engagement. Post as the founder, not the brand.

8. **Track LinkedIn as a Channel**: Know your revenue attribution. Both experts can point to specific revenue from LinkedIn.

9. **The Long Game**: Some of Alex's best clients took months or years to convert. Consistency compounds.

10. **Help First, Sell Never**: Both experts' core philosophy is about helping publicly. Sales conversations happen naturally when trust is established.
