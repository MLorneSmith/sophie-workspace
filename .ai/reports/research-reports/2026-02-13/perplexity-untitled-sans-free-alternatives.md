## Query Summary
Searched for the closest free/open-source alternatives to Untitled Sans by Klim Type Foundry. Untitled Sans is a neo-grotesque sans-serif designed by Kris Sowersby (2017), deliberately crafted to look "quotidian" and common with no discerning characteristics, low contrast, and a neutral contemporary feel.

## Top 7 Free Alternatives (Ranked by Similarity)

### 1. Inter (Google Fonts) -- Closest Match (~88% similarity)
- **Why similar**: Geometric structure, low contrast, neutral neo-grotesque feel, contemporary design
- **Differences**: More explicitly optimized for digital interfaces and small-size legibility; slightly taller x-height; includes OpenType features like contextual alternates for UI
- **Availability**: Google Fonts, open-source (SIL OFL)

### 2. Public Sans (Google Fonts)
- **Why similar**: Neo-grotesque with low contrast and an intentionally unassuming, "everyday" appearance -- matches Untitled Sans's "quotidian" design philosophy most closely
- **Differences**: Originally designed for US government (USWDS); slightly more mechanical in some weights; narrower character width
- **Availability**: Google Fonts, open-source (SIL OFL)

### 3. General Sans (Fontshare)
- **Why similar**: Versatile neo-grotesque with neutral tone, clean geometry, and minimal distinctive features; similar weight range
- **Differences**: Slightly more contemporary/trendy feel; popular in startup/tech branding which can make it feel less "invisible"
- **Availability**: Fontshare (Indian Type Foundry), free for commercial use

### 4. Switzer (Fontshare)
- **Why similar**: Neo-grotesque sans-serif with clean proportions, low contrast, and neutral character
- **Differences**: Subtly wider letterforms; more weights available; draws more from Swiss grotesque tradition
- **Availability**: Fontshare (Indian Type Foundry), free for commercial use

### 5. DM Sans (Google Fonts)
- **Why similar**: Low-contrast geometric sans-serif designed for smaller text sizes; clean, minimalist aesthetic
- **Differences**: Subtly warmer with influences from Poppins; geometric rather than neo-grotesque classification; slightly rounder forms
- **Availability**: Google Fonts, open-source (SIL OFL)

### 6. IBM Plex Sans (Google Fonts)
- **Why similar**: Neutral grotesk with restrained personality; low contrast; broad weight range; functional clarity
- **Differences**: Adds subtle corporate friendliness and tech heritage; part of a superfamily (Serif, Mono, Sans Condensed); slightly more character in terminals
- **Availability**: Google Fonts, open-source (SIL OFL)

### 7. Epilogue (Google Fonts)
- **Why similar**: Variable sans-serif with 9 weights; low-contrast, modern geometric proportions; contemporary feel
- **Differences**: More weight modulation via variable font technology; slightly more personality in italic forms
- **Availability**: Google Fonts, open-source (SIL OFL)

### Honorable Mentions
- **Uncut Sans**: Noted as a direct free alternative, sometimes paired with Untitled Sans
- **Mona Sans** (GitHub): Versatile for product design, neo-grotesque
- **Outfit** (Google Fonts): Clean and modern but leans more geometric/stylized than Untitled Sans's deliberately bland intent

## Key Design Characteristics of Untitled Sans (for comparison)
- **Classification**: Neo-grotesque sans-serif
- **Designer**: Kris Sowersby (Klim Type Foundry, 2017)
- **Philosophy**: Designed to look "quotidian" -- common, everyday, with no discerning characteristics
- **Contrast**: Low stroke contrast
- **Structure**: Subtle humanist touches within neo-grotesque framework
- **Weights**: 5 weights with matching italics
- **Testing method**: Sowersby tested by embedding in an ePub of Orwell's 1984, removing any details that stood out during reading

## Key Takeaways
- Inter and Public Sans are the two closest free matches on Google Fonts
- General Sans and Switzer on Fontshare are strong alternatives with slightly more contemporary character
- For maximum "invisibility" (matching Untitled Sans's design philosophy), Public Sans is the best match
- For overall versatility and screen optimization, Inter is the safest choice
- All recommended fonts are free for commercial use under open-source licenses
