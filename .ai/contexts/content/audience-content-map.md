## Primary Personas

### 1. Solo Consultant

**Who they are:** Independent consultants, freelancers, fractional executives. 5-20+ years experience, often ex-Big 4 or corporate strategy. Revenue $150K-$500K+.

**Their world:**
- Compete with Big 4 on deliverable quality
- Time is money — every hour on slides is an hour not billing
- Clients judge them by presentation quality
- No team to delegate to — they do everything themselves

**What keeps them up at night:**
- "I spend 15-20 hours per pitch deck. My competitor spent 3."
- "My decks don't look like McKinsey's."
- "I lost a deal to a 3-person team from a Big 4 firm."
- "I know my content but struggle to structure it."

**Primary Content Pillars:**
- Methodology Mastery (60%)
- Career & Business Impact (25%)
- Problem-Solving & Teardowns (10%)
- AI + Methodology (5%)

**What Content Resonates:**

**Blog Posts:**
- "Competing with Big 4 Quality as a Solo Consultant"
- "15 Hours Per Deck → 5 Hours Per Deck: The Consultant's Time Problem"
- "How to Structure a Client Deliverable (Consulting Format)"
- "The Methodology Behind $500/Hour Consulting Decks"

**LinkedIn Posts:**
- Quick tips they can use immediately
- Consultant-specific problems (client deliverables, pitch decks)
- Competitive advantages and differentiation
- "I saw this in a deck recently..." stories

**Emails:**
- Time-saving techniques
- Client deliverable quality
- Winning more pitches
- ROI of methodology investment

**Outbound:**
- "Stop redoing client work" (partner time problem)
- "Your deliverables vs. McKinsey's" (quality comparison)
- "15 hours you can't bill" (time pain)

**Value Props That Work:**
- "Deliverables that compete with Big 4 quality — at solo prices"
- "Win more pitches with structured, executive-ready presentations"
- "15 hours per deck → 5 hours per deck"
- "The methodology behind $500/hour consulting decks"

**Proof Points That Convince:**
- "Developed from methodology used at McKinsey, BCG, Bain"
- "Taught at Seneca College"
- "Used by professionals who pitch to Fortune 500 clients"

**Keywords & Topics:**
- Consulting presentation structure
- Client deliverable quality
- Pitch deck structure
- McKinsey presentation style
- Time-saving presentation techniques

**Content to Avoid:**
- Corporate-specific content (performance reviews, executive presence)
- Academic/theoretical content without practical application
- Motivational/career-advancement content (wrong focus for consultants)

### 2. Boutique Consultancy

**Who they are:** Small consulting firms (2-20 people). Partner(s) + associates/analysts. Revenue $500K-$10M. Compete on quality of thinking and presentation.

**Their world:**
- Need consistent deliverables across the entire team
- Partners spend too much time fixing associate work
- Can't afford Big 4 training programs ($50K+ for workshops)
- Scaling without diluting quality

**What keeps them up at night:**
- "Our deliverables are inconsistent — partner decks vs. associate decks."
- "I spend my time redoing work instead of reviewing it."
- "A client compared our associate's deck to a McKinsey deliverable."
- "We grew from 4 to 15 people and quality cratered."

**Primary Content Pillars:**
- Methodology Mastery (50%)
- Career & Business Impact (30%)
- Problem-Solving & Teardowns (15%)
- AI + Methodology (5%)

**What Content Resonates:**

**Blog Posts:**
- "How to Create Consistent Deliverables Across Your Consulting Team"
- "Stop Redoing. Start Reviewing. The Partner's Time Problem."
- "McKinsey Training at Boutique Prices"
- "4 Hours of Training → 5+ Hours Saved Per Deliverable"

**LinkedIn Posts:**
- Team consistency challenges
- Partner time savings
- Scaling without quality loss
- Before/after associate work examples

**Emails:**
- Business case for team training
- ROI of methodology investment
- Partner time savings
- Firm-wide consistency

**Outbound:**
- "Why your associate decks don't look like your partner decks"
- "The hidden cost of fixing junior work"
- "Train once, benefit on every engagement"

**Value Props That Work:**
- "Consistent, executive-ready deliverables across your entire team"
- "Stop redoing. Start reviewing."
- "Train once, benefit on every engagement"
- "The training program you can't afford from Big 4"

**Proof Points That Convince:**
- "Institutional curriculum at Seneca College"
- "Volume pricing for teams"
- "Self-paced — train without partner involvement"
- "4 hours of training → 5+ hours saved per deliverable"

**Keywords & Topics:**
- Consulting team training
- Consistent deliverable quality
- Partner review time
- Associate training methodology
- Firm-wide presentation standards

**Content to Avoid:**
- Individual-focused content (career advancement, personal skills)
- Generic motivation — they care about business impact, not personal growth
- Public speaking/stage content — they're about document creation

## Persona-Specific Voice Guidelines

### Solo Consultant Voice
- Peer-to-peer respect ("you know your content")
- Competitive awareness (acknowledge Big 4 comparison)
- Time-focused language (quantify hours saved)
- Client-centric ("your Fortune 500 client", "the PE partner")

### Boutique Consultancy Voice
- Business-focused ROI perspective
- Team language ("your associates", "partner review")
- Scaling awareness ("what worked at 4 breaks at 15")
- Comparison to alternatives (Big 4 training costs)

### Corporate Professional Voice
- Career-focused ("get promoted", "executive presence")
- Empathetic to corporate dynamics
- Confirms their expertise ("Your work is solid")
- Acknowledges politics and power dynamics

### Entrepreneur/Founder Voice
- Urgency and stakes ("this deck gets you funded")
- Investor perspective ("what VCs actually look at")
- Idea-focused ("your vision deserves this deck")
- Data-backed ("which slides get the most attention")

## Seasonal/Contextual Adjustments

### Q1 (New Year, Resolutions)
- Corporate: Career planning, "skill up for promotions"
- Consultants: "Win more pitches this year"

### Q2 (Strategy Planning)
- Boutique: Firm planning, team training
- Corporate: Executive presentations, mid-year reviews

### Q3 (Busy Season)
- Solo Consultant: Time-saving techniques, efficiency
- All: Quick wins, practical tips (less theory)

### Q4 (Year-End Reviews, Bonuses)
- Corporate: Year-end presentations, making your case
- Consultants: Client deliverables, closing deals
- Entrepreneurs: Year-end fundraising

## Anti-Pattern: What NOT to Write for Each Persona

| Persona | Avoid | Why |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Solo Consultant | Corporate career language, motivation | They're business owners, not employees |
| Boutique | Individual focus, generic tips | They care about business impact, not personal growth |
| Corporate | "client" language, pricing per seat | Wrong context, many self-fund |
| Entrepreneur | Generic "presentation tips" | Too broad, they need specific pitch deck help |
