## Summary

LinkedIn content follows a dual-pillar system: **Format Pillars** (how we write) and **Theme Pillars** (what we write about). Every post maps to one format pillar AND one theme pillar.

**Core principle:** "Engagement and leads are two different games." (Anton Maker) — We optimize for leads, not likes.

---

## Format Pillars (Anton Maker's 4-Pillar System)

| Pillar | Weight | Purpose | What It Looks Like |
|--------|--------|---------|-------------------|
| **Tactical** | 40% | Actionable advice, tips, how-tos | "3 slides every consulting proposal needs" |
| **Transformational** | 20% | Case studies, before/after results | "How [firm] cut proposal time from 2 weeks to 2 days" |
| **Insightful** | 20% | Hot takes, contrarian opinions | "Why your pitch deck is losing you deals" |
| **Personal** | 20% | Stories, behind-the-scenes, founder journey | "What I learned about presentations in 20 years at Mastercard" |

## Post Formulas (Anton Maker)

Five proven structures that book meetings:

1. **The Transformation Post** — Specific metric before → after + counterintuitive approach
2. **The Expensive Mistake Post** — What we tried → Why it failed → What actually worked
3. **The Diagnosis Post** — "If [situation], you don't have [symptom], you have [root cause]"
4. **The Hidden Cost Post** — Quantify a cost they're not tracking + solution impact
5. **The A vs B Post** — Common approach vs your approach with results comparison

## LinkedIn-Specific Formatting (Gio Laga)

- **O(1) scannability** — scannable in one glance
- **Short paragraphs** — 1-2 sentences max
- **Line breaks between paragraphs** — white space is your friend
- **Hook in first 2 lines** — must earn the "see more" click
- **No external links in post body** — kills reach. Put links in first comment.
- **No hashtags** (or max 3 at the very end)
- **Emojis sparingly** — 1-2 max, not emoji soup
